HEADLINE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The June projections invert the committee's directional bias. In March the
median dot saw a CUT (3.4%) and NOT ONE participant projected a 2026 hike.
In June the median sits at 3.8% — at/above the current 3.625% midpoint — and
NINE of 18 participants pencil in at least one 2026 hike. The driver is a
single variable: the 2026 headline PCE forecast jumped +0.9 (2.7% → 3.6%),
core +0.6 (2.7% → 3.3%). Growth is slightly softer and unemployment slightly
LOWER, so this is purely an inflation repricing, not a labor/growth story —
the committee is choosing to hold against inflation despite an admittedly
softening labor market. This ratifies the post-April hawkish drift in the
speeches and the three April-29 dissents against the easing bias.

KEY READ ON THE RATE PATH:
• 2026 median 3.4 → 3.8: a +0.4 jump. In March the median implied roughly one
  CUT from the current 3.625% midpoint by year-end. In June the median is
  ABOVE the current rate — the committee's central expectation flipped from
  "cut once" to "hold, with a real chance of a hike."
• Longer-run rate UNCHANGED at 3.1%. The committee did NOT raise its estimate
  of neutral. It is saying policy must stay HIGHER-FOR-LONGER relative to an
  unchanged neutral, not that neutral moved. The intended degree of
  restrictiveness roughly doubled: 2026 median minus longer-run was +0.3 in
  March (3.4 vs 3.1) and is +0.7 in June (3.8 vs 3.1). [Per the CLAUDE.md
  neutral-rate precision rule: this resolves that the higher path is a
  restrictiveness call, not a neutral-rate re-estimate.]

THE ONE FEWER DOT (19 → 18) — what it does and does NOT explain
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CONFIRMED: 19 participants in March, 18 in June — one fewer, as flagged.

Accounting [INFERENCE — flag for analyst confirmation]:
• Board of Governors is unchanged at 7. Warsh was sworn in as Chair and took
  the seat Miran vacated; Powell stayed on as a governor and still votes (he
  is in the June statement vote list). So the net −1 is NOT a board seat.
• The net-missing dot is therefore a regional president who did not submit —
  most likely ATLANTA (Bostic retired Feb 2026; interim president, not yet
  posting speeches). Confirm against the official PDF.

SEPARATELY — a hawkish composition SWAP inside the board: Miran (the
committee's deepest dove — the lone 2.625% dot in March and the lone March-18
cut dissenter) departed and his seat is now Warsh (hawkish). Dove-out / hawk-in
tilts the board's dots up independent of anyone changing their mind.

CRITICAL — do NOT over-attribute to composition: the +0.4 median jump and the
collapse of the dovish tail (5 sub-current dots → 1) CANNOT be explained by
losing or swapping one participant. The median is set by the 9th–10th dots,
far from the tail; roughly 13–14 participants moved their own dots up. This is
a genuine, broad, inflation-driven repricing. The lost/swapped dot reinforces
the hawkish optics but is a second-order effect, not the cause.

EVALUATION AGAINST THE SPEECHES (since the April 29 FOMC)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The SEP confirms — and formalizes into the committee's central forecast — the
hawkish drift already visible in the speech record:

• THE APRIL-29 DISSENTERS WON. Logan, Hammack, and Kashkari dissented Apr 29
  against the easing-bias language. The June statement DROPPED the easing bias
  AND the dots moved up two steps. Their dissent was a leading indicator of the
  committee's center of gravity, exactly as the bottom-up language cycle predicts.

• WALLER (Apr 17, "sequence of transitory shocks"; first voter to say he'd hold
  even if the labor market weakens). The SEP's hold-despite-lower-unemployment,
  inflation-driven path is the numerical expression of that framework.

• POWELL (Apr 29 Q&A): two explicit pre-cut conditions named ("backside of the
  energy shock" AND "progress on tariffs") plus a "next two quarters" deadline.
  The median dot sees NO 2026 cut — fully consistent.

• PAULSON, median voter: explicit inflation-first bar — "if inflation is above
  2 percent and has been for some time, I would be more cautious." With PCE
  penciled at 3.6% for 2026, that bar implies no cuts; the 3.75% median dot
  sits right where her framework points.

• GOOLSBEE (non-voter): "not obvious our interest rate policy is even
  restrictive" — consistent with the upper dot cluster (4.125%, 4.375%).

THE TENSION TO WATCH (bottom-up language-cycle gap):
The dots are MORE hawkish than the statement. Half the committee pencils a 2026
hike, yet the statement moved only from an easing bias to NEUTRAL ("attentive
to the risks to both sides"), not to a tightening bias. Individual hawkish
positioning has NOT yet consolidated into committee guidance. Two readings,
both worth holding:
  (a) Leading indicator — if inflation stays ~3.6%, a tightening bias could
      surface in a future statement; the dots front-run the language.
  (b) Warsh anchoring consensus — the new Chair's first meeting produced a
      UNANIMOUS hold with neutral guidance despite a hawkish dot field,
      suggesting he is restraining the hawks into consensus rather than
      ratifying them. Watch his press conference (5pm) for which way he leans.
