INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation remains somewhat elevated." [Jan 28 stmt]
  → Cook: "PCE price index rose 2.9 percent...core...3 percent.
    Progress on inflation essentially stalled in 2025."

Own language — KEY:
  "I see risks as tilted toward higher inflation."
  "Until I see stronger evidence that inflation is moving
   sustainably back down to target, that is where my focus
   will be, in the absence of unexpected changes in the
   labor market."
  → Explicit hawk lean. Cook is not symmetric — she places
    more weight on upside inflation risk than downside labor.

  "After nearly five years of above-target inflation, it is
   essential that we maintain our credibility."
  → Credibility framing: she is willing to stay restrictive
    longer to protect the inflation anchor.

  "I am watching the clock — the longer inflation remains above
   target, the greater the probability that higher inflation
   will become entrenched in expectations."
  → Time-based hawkish pressure: prolonged above-target
    inflation makes her more willing to tolerate labor risk.

SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    HAWKISH LEAN — inflation-first, patient hold

Directional:   Cook is at the hawk end of the voter spectrum.
  She explicitly tilts toward inflation risk, uses credibility
  framing, and sets no timeline or conditions for cuts. Her
  labor concern is real but subordinate to inflation vigilance.

Language watch:
  • "Risks tilted toward higher inflation" — her personal risk
    framing; more asymmetric than the committee's "dual risk"
    language. Not yet echoed by others.
  • "Five years of above-target inflation" — credibility clock.
    Barr and Paulson also use this framing. Track if it spreads.
