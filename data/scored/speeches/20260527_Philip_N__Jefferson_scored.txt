GROWTH
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Economic activity has been expanding at a solid pace." [Apr 29 stmt]
  → Jefferson: "Recent economic growth in the U.S. has been solid."
    Near-verbatim echo of statement characterization.

Own language:
  "...though I expect a more modest pace of growth this year as
   households face high energy costs."
  → New addition vs. Apr 7: Jefferson names energy costs as a growth
    headwind for household spending — explicitly flagging demand-side
    drag. Apr 7 did not include a growth deceleration expectation.
    "I am watching whether higher energy prices will start to weigh on
    consumer spending." — active monitoring signal.

Shift vs. prior speech (Apr 7):  SLIGHT DOVISH on growth — Apr 7 had no
  explicit growth deceleration expectation; May 27 adds "more modest pace"
  with energy cost identified as mechanism. Not a policy-relevant
  concern in isolation, but meaningful as backdrop for inflation framing.
Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Apr 7 = also pre-FOMC baseline): same as above.

INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation remains somewhat elevated." [Apr 29 stmt — implicit]
  → Jefferson does not directly echo the statement phrase; he gives
    a more specific characterization.

Own language — KEY:
  "Disinflation in the U.S. stalled over the preceding year, largely
   because of increased tariffs."
  → Consistent with Apr 7 characterization.

  "In recent months, inflation moved notably higher because of higher
   energy costs."
  → HAWKISH UPGRADE vs. Apr 7. Apr 7 language: "recent increase in
    energy prices...will apply some upward pressure on headline
    inflation, at least in the near term." May 27 language: inflation
    has already "moved notably higher" — present tense, confirmed
    deterioration, not prospective pressure. This is a meaningful
    strengthening of the inflation description.

  "I expect inflation to decline later this year as the effects of
   tariffs and the energy shock wane..."
  → Jefferson adopts the two-shock conditional structure: disinflation
    depends on BOTH tariff pass-through AND energy shock waning.
    This mirrors the two pre-cut conditions Powell named in the
    Apr 29 Q&A ("backside of the energy shock" AND "progress on
    tariffs"). Jefferson is aligning his inflation baseline with the
    Powell-announced framework. [Inference: this language does not
    appear to be accidental alignment; it echoes the Troika framework
    set in the Apr 29 press conference.]

  "...but I view risks around my inflation outlook as tilted to the
   upside."
  → Direct carryover from Apr 7 ("upside risk to my inflation
    forecast"). Now durable across two speeches. May 27 formulation
    slightly broadened: "risks around my inflation outlook" (Apr 7:
    "risk to my inflation forecast") — same substance.

  "I am firmly committed to returning inflation to the Federal Open
   Market Committee's 2 percent target."
  → Explicit inflation commitment, placed at the opening of the policy
    paragraph — unusual positioning that signals priority ranking.
    Not present as a standalone commitment sentence in Apr 7.

Shift vs. prior speech (Apr 7):  HAWKISH — "moved notably higher" is
  a confirmed deterioration vs. Apr 7's prospective "upward pressure";
  two-shock conditional structure is now explicit where Apr 7 only
  named tariffs as the primary driver of resumed disinflation.
Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Apr 7):  HAWKISH — same as above.

MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "well positioned to respond to a range of outcomes" [Apr 29 stmt]
  → Jefferson: "this policy stance leaves us well positioned to
    respond to economic developments based on the incoming data,
    the evolving outlook, and the balance of risks."
    Mirrors the committee's holding formula closely.

Own language:
  "I have not prejudged the next meeting and look forward to
   engaging with my colleagues about the policy necessary to
   best achieve our dual-mandate goals."
  → "Have not prejudged" is a deliberate optionality statement —
    more open-ended than Apr 7's unqualified "well positioned"
    formula. In context of the committee's emerging hawkish tilt
    (Waller May 22, Cook May 27 explicit hike signals), this
    language does NOT rule out discussion of tightening at June 18.
    It is also not a hike signal — Jefferson does not echo
    Waller's "prepared to raise rates" framing.
    [Inference: "not prejudged" may be Jefferson's way of keeping
    committee optionality without endorsing the hike direction.
    Watch for whether he adds directional guidance before June 6 blackout.]

Cut signal status: ABSENT — no "further reductions" language;
  not carried forward from any prior Jefferson speech.
Hike signal status: ABSENT — no "prepared to raise rates" or
  equivalent. But not ruled out by "not prejudged" language.

Shift vs. prior speech (Apr 7):  SLIGHT HAWKISH LEAN — Apr 7
  projected more certainty in the hold formula ("confident our
  current policy stance is well-positioned"); May 27 adds the
  "not prejudged" qualifier, which is structurally more open.
  The explicit inflation-first commitment ("firmly committed")
  is also new emphasis.
Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Apr 7):  SLIGHT HAWKISH LEAN — same as above.

SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    HAWKISH LEAN
               Jefferson's first post-April-29 economic outlook
               speech upgrades the inflation description from
               prospective pressure to confirmed deterioration
               ("moved notably higher"). Labor is stable with
               maintained downside risks. Growth is solid but
               includes a new energy-cost headwind. Policy hold
               confirmed with "not prejudged" language that keeps
               the door open without endorsing the Waller/Cook
               hike signal. The two-shock conditional (tariffs AND
               energy) for disinflation resumption is now explicit
               — Troika-level adoption of the framework Powell
               named Apr 29.

Language watch:
  • "Inflation moved notably higher" — HAWKISH upgrade vs. Jefferson's
    prior language; first time Jefferson uses "moved higher" for
    inflation (prior: "upward pressure"). Watch for Williams to adopt
    or contradict.
  • "Firmly committed to returning inflation to the 2 percent target" —
    new explicit inflation commitment framing; added emphasis vs. prior
    speeches. Dispersed stage; watch for spread.
  • Two-shock conditional ("as the effects of tariffs and the energy
    shock wane") — confirms Jefferson aligning with Powell Apr 29
    framework; now Troika consensus on the precondition structure.
  • "Have not prejudged the next meeting" — optionality language;
    weaker than an active policy lean but notable for a Troika
    member this close to blackout. If it spreads to Williams or
    Warsh, signals June 18 is genuinely live for directional change.
