INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation remains somewhat elevated." [Jan 28 stmt]
  → Collins: "Even with the economy growing near capacity,
    and limited price pressures from the labor market,
    inflation remained elevated."

Own language — KEY:
  "My outlook has demand exerting some upward pressure on
   prices, slowing the return of inflation to 2 percent."
  → Mild hawkish lean: demand is not cooling fast enough
    to get inflation to target quickly.

  "I do not see an urgency for additional policy adjustments,
   and I will be looking for clear evidence that inflation is
   moving durably toward the 2 percent target — something
   that might occur only over the second half of the year."
  → "Second half of the year" is her timeline for when
    evidence might appear. Patient framing.

  "Considerable economic uncertainty remains, exacerbated
   by the outbreak of hostilities in the Middle East."
  → Notes geopolitical risk as a complication.

SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    NEUTRAL-PATIENT — comfortable hold, no urgency

Directional:   Collins sits in the middle of the voter spectrum:
  not as dovish as Bowman, not as hawkish as Cook. Her "mildly
  restrictive" rate characterization and H2 evidence timeline
  suggest she could support cuts if disinflation resumes as
  expected in the second half of 2026.

Language watch:
  • "Mildly restrictive" — her personal rate label. Between
    Bowman's "moderately restrictive" and Logan's "near neutral."
  • "Second half of the year" — her disinflation evidence
    timeline. Watch for this to compress or extend.

Analyst flag: NO — straightforward neutral-patient stance.
