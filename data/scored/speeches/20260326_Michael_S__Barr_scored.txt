INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation remains somewhat elevated." [Mar 18 stmt]
  → Barr: "Core inflation...likely was 3 percent in February,
    about where it was a year ago."

Own language — KEY:
  "We have had five years now of inflation at elevated levels,
   and near-term inflation expectations have risen again, so
   I am particularly concerned that yet another price shock
   could increase longer-term inflation expectations."
  → Five-year credibility framing + rising near-term
    expectations = hawkish concern. Explicitly worried about
    expectation de-anchoring from the energy shock.

  "We need to be especially vigilant."
  → Strong language. More explicit than the committee's
    standard "attentive" phrasing.

  "Nonhousing services inflation has also remained elevated.
   Core inflation...likely was 3 percent in February, about
   where it was a year ago."
  → No progress on services inflation — reinforces his
    hawkish concern.

SUMMARY

Net signal:    HAWKISH LEAN — inflation vigilance, energy shock
               elevates his concern about expectation anchoring

Directional:   Barr is at the hawkish end of the voter spectrum
  on inflation (alongside Cook and Paulson). His "especially
  vigilant" language and five-year credibility framing place
  him in the group most concerned about the energy shock
  becoming embedded. Labor fragility is acknowledged but
  subordinate to inflation concern.

Language watch:
  • "Five years of elevated inflation" + "near-term expectations
    rising again" — credibility clock language. Also used by
    Cook and Paulson. Potential committee-wide theme.
  • "Especially vigilant" — stronger than standard "attentive";
    watch if this spreads to Troika.
