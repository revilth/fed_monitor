LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Job gains have remained low, and the unemployment rate has
   shown some signs of stabilization." [Jan 28 stmt]
  → Williams: "unemployment rate...4.3 percent...back to where
    it was in July 2025. Job growth...have stabilized in
    recent months."

Own verdict:
  "Despite solid economic growth in 2025, we saw a gradual
   softening of the labor market through much of the year.
   In recent months, however, there have been promising signs
   of stabilization."
  → Hopeful framing: stabilization after softening. Not fragile.

Own caveat:
  "The low hiring rate, along with an increase in long-term
   unemployment, may be contributing to a somewhat more
   pessimistic perception among households than other
   indicators of the labor market might suggest."
  → Notes the low-hire/perception gap. Same observation
    as Goolsbee (Feb 24) but less analytical.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Jan 12): MILD POSITIVE — Jan 12 was more
  cautious; Mar 3 sees "promising signs of stabilization."
  Slight dovish lean (labor concerns easing).

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation remains somewhat elevated." [Jan 28 stmt]
  → Williams: "tariffs have contributed around one half to
    three quarters of a percentage point to the current
    inflation rate of about 3 percent."

Own language — KEY:
  "There are no signs of significant second-round effects
   from tariffs. No global supply-chain bottlenecks have
   emerged. Wage growth has remained stable at levels
   consistent with price stability."
  → Three-part dovish anchor: no second-round effects, no
    supply chain disruptions, no wage inflation. This is the
    most complete articulation of the committee's "tariffs
    are one-time" framework by any official.

  "I expect the tariffs largely to have one-off effects on
   prices. Therefore, I anticipate inflation to start coming
   back down later this year."
  → Confident H2 disinflation forecast. No hedging.

Own forecast:
  "I expect overall inflation to come in at around 2-1/2
   percent in 2026, then fall to 2 percent in 2027."
  → Specific numeric forecast. 2.5% for 2026, 2% for 2027.
    This will be revised upward in Mar 30 after energy shock.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Jan 12): SLIGHTLY MORE OPTIMISTIC — Jan 12
  was more cautious on tariff pass-through timing. Mar 3
  is confident in H2 disinflation. Mild dovish lean.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  → Williams: "With these actions, the risks to achieving our
    maximum employment and price stability goals are now in
    better balance."

Own language — CUT SIGNAL:
  "If inflation follows the path I expect, further reductions
   in the federal funds rate will eventually be warranted to
   prevent monetary policy from inadvertently becoming more
   restrictive."
  → MOST IMPORTANT SENTENCE. Explicit cut signal with
    conditional: if disinflation proceeds, cuts needed to
    prevent policy from becoming inadvertently tighter as
    inflation falls. This is the committee's most forward-
    leaning statement from any Troika member in 2026.

Cut signal status: PRESENT — explicit and conditional cut signal.
  This language will be retracted by Mar 30.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Jan 12): DOVISH — Jan 12 did not contain
  an explicit cut signal. Mar 3 introduces "further reductions
  will eventually be warranted." This is the peak dovishness
  of Williams' 2026 Troika communications.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    DOVISH — most explicit Troika cut signal in 2026

Directional:   Williams' Mar 3 speech is the high-water mark
  of Troika dovishness in 2026. It will be reversed by Mar 30
  after the Middle East energy shock. The retraction of "further
  reductions will eventually be warranted" between Mar 3 and
  Mar 30 is the single most important Troika language shift of
  the year to date.

Language watch:
  • "Further reductions in the federal funds rate will eventually
    be warranted" ← CUT SIGNAL — watch for retraction.
  • "No signs of significant second-round effects" — this phrase
    appears in all subsequent Williams speeches. Its continued
    presence is the dovish anchor; its disappearance would be
    a major hawkish signal.
  • "Risks now in better balance" — standard committee holding
    formula from Jan 28 FOMC.

Analyst flag: YES
  → Did media or markets price in the Williams cut signal from
    Mar 3? If so, the Mar 30 retraction may have been a
    deliberate correction of a market overreaction.
