INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  Dec 2025: "Inflation has eased... but remains somewhat elevated"
  → Jefferson: "inflation remains at a level that is above readings
    consistent with our inflation target."
  → Close paraphrase of statement language.

Own language — KEY:
  "Core goods inflation... rose sharply, to 1.4 percent... at least
   in part reflecting increased tariffs filtering through to certain
   goods prices." — Tariffs acknowledged as cause, but framed as
   "at least in part" — open to non-tariff contributors.
  "It is a reasonable base case that the effects of tariffs on
   inflation will not be long-lasting — effectively, a one-time
   shift in the price level." — Same look-through framing as Williams
   (Jan 12). Tariffs: one-time level shift.
  "Inflation will resume a path toward our goal" — forward path
   constructive.
  Shelter: "continued to decline"; core services ex-shelter: "on a
  downward trend, albeit on a somewhat bumpier path."

  Calibration note: Jefferson does not offer a specific inflation
  forecast (contrast with Williams' 2.75–3% peak / 2% by 2027).
  His framing is more directional than quantified.

SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    NEUTRAL / SLIGHTLY HAWKISH LEAN — Jefferson's "at
               neutral" rate characterization is modestly more hawkish
               than Williams' "closer to neutral." Combined with his
               explicit acknowledgment of elevated downside labor risks
               (somewhat dovish) and tariff look-through (dovish), the
               net stance is balanced but with a hawkish tilt on the
               rate level framing.

Language watch:
  • "At neutral" — Jefferson's characterization; watch whether
    Williams adopts this phrase in subsequent speeches. If both
    Troika members say rates are "at neutral," the case for cuts
    on a restriction basis collapses.
  • "Downside risks to employment have risen" — first Troika use
    of this phrase in 2026; tracks to the Apr 7 scored Jefferson
    speech where it became "susceptible to adverse shocks."
  • "One-time shift in the price level" — Jefferson/Williams both
    using this exact look-through formulation. Watch for it in
    statement language.

Analyst flag:  YES
  → Jefferson says rates are "at neutral"; Williams (Jan 12) says
    "closer to neutral." This is a meaningful divergence within
    the Troika. Analyst to assess whether this reflects a genuine
    difference in Jefferson's neutral-rate estimate vs. Williams',
    or whether one of them updated their view after the other's
    speech. If Jefferson's "at neutral" characterization holds
    through the Jan 28 FOMC, it implies no rate cut bias at the
    Troika level.
