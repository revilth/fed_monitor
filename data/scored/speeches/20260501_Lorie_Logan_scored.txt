LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"The labor market has been stable, with low unemployment and payroll
job gains keeping pace with labor force growth."
→ Stable, balanced read. No downside risk flagged. Slightly more

Direction: neutral-to-hawkish — stable, not a concern for her.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Own language — KEY:
  "I am increasingly concerned about how long it will take inflation
   to return all the way to the FOMC's 2 percent target. PCE price
   inflation has exceeded 2 percent for more than five years."
  "Even before recent increases in the prices of energy and other
   commodities, those measures had been running meaningfully above
   2 percent, leaving doubts about how long it will take inflation
   to return to target."
  → CRITICAL: Logan explicitly separates pre-existing inflation
    from the energy shock. Underlying inflation was already a
    concern before the conflict — energy is additive. This directly
    contradicts the statement's energy-attribution framing.
  "The conflict in the Middle East raises the prospect of prolonged
   or repeated supply disruptions that could create further
   inflationary pressures."
Direction: HAWKISH — pre-existing inflation concern; energy additive
  not the primary cause.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dissent rationale: The "additional adjustments" language "implies the
  next rate change will most likely reduce the target range again."
  "I disagree with that assessment of the policy outlook."
  "In light of the two-sided risks to monetary policy, I believed
   the FOMC should not give forward guidance implying a bias toward
   rate cuts at this time."
  "Depending on which scenario materializes, it could plausibly be
   appropriate for the FOMC's next rate change to be either an
   increase or a cut."
  → Explicit symmetric framing: next move could be hike or cut.
    Logan also frames forward guidance as a policy tool in itself:
    "When the FOMC gives forward guidance... that guidance is an
    important policy tool. It influences financial conditions and
    the economy." She is objecting to the mechanism of the guidance,
    not just its direction.

Cut signal status: EXPLICITLY OPPOSED — symmetric guidance called for.
Direction: HAWKISH — explicit two-sided risk, guidance-as-policy-tool
  framing.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Feb 10): UNCHANGED in direction, more specific
  in rationale. Feb 10 established "very close to neutral" and
  inflation concern; May 1 adds the forward guidance critique and
  the "five years above 2%" duration framing.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    HAWKISH — consistent with Feb 10 stance; adds explicit
               forward guidance critique and pre-existing inflation concern.

Language watch:
  • "Meaningfully above 2% even before energy" — isolates pre-existing
    inflation from the energy shock; undermines the look-through case.
  • "Forward guidance is an important policy tool" — frames the easing
    bias debate at the mechanism level, not just the direction level.
    This is a sophisticated objection that is harder to dismiss.
  • "Either an increase or a cut" — symmetric language, not just
    "neutral." Hike is explicitly on the table for Logan.

Analyst flag: NO — consistent with established Logan stance.
