INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation remains somewhat elevated." [Jan 28 stmt]
  → Logan: "PCE inflation...2.8 percent...above the
    Committee's definition of price stability of 2 percent
    for nearly five years."

Own language — KEY:
  "I am not yet fully confident inflation is heading all the
   way back to 2 percent."
  "I see several reasons why progress could be slower than
   expected."
  → Hawkish uncertainty flag: not confident in disinflation
    completion. This is more cautious than Jefferson's
    "tariffs are temporary, disinflation resumes."

  "After last year's rate cuts, it is uncertain how restrictive
   policy currently is."
  → KEY PHRASE: Questions whether rates are even restrictive.
    Logan is the first official to explicitly raise the
    restrictiveness question in a policy speech.

MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed: none (non-voter; less direct
  reference to FOMC statement language)

Own language — KEY:
  "The current real fed funds rate...now sits squarely within
   the range of neutral rate estimates."
  "Our current stance of policy may be very close to neutral
   and providing little restraint on economic activity
   and inflation."
  → Most hawkish neutral-rate characterization in 2026.
    "Very close to neutral" means cuts are not obviously
    warranted — the restriction argument is gone.

  "Right now, I am more worried about inflation remaining
   stubbornly high."
  → Explicit asymmetric risk statement toward inflation.

  "If instead we see inflation coming down but with further
   material cooling in the labor market, cutting rates again
   could become appropriate. But right now, I am more worried
   about inflation remaining stubbornly high."
  → Sets her bar: cuts require labor market cooling AND
    inflation progress simultaneously. Very high bar.

Cut signal status: ABSENT — explicit inflation-first framing.

SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    HAWKISH — most hawkish regional president speech
               in 2026; leading indicator of hawkish voter sentiment

Directional:   Logan provides the sharpest articulation of the
  hawkish analytical case: labor is stable, policy may already
  be near neutral, inflation progress is uncertain, and she is
  more worried about inflation than labor. This is the
  committee's hawkish intellectual pole.

Language watch:
  • "Policy may be very close to neutral" — Logan's key phrase.
    If this spreads to voters (especially Jefferson or Williams),
    it removes the mechanical case for cuts even if inflation
    falls gradually. Goolsbee (Feb 24) adopts similar language.
  • "Not yet fully confident inflation is heading to 2%" — her
    personal inflation confidence bar. Higher than committee avg.

Analyst flag: YES
  → Logan has the highest analytical credibility of any regional
    voter (former NY Fed markets desk head). Note: Logan is a Just
    Voter in 2026 — confirmed by her April 29 dissent against easing
    bias language. Her "near neutral" framing is the hawkish end of
    the actual voter coalition. Analyst to assess: if Troika officials
    adopt Logan's neutral-rate view, what does that imply for the
    cut timeline?
