──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

GROWTH / LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pre-conflict baseline: "The labor market appeared lukewarm but largely
  stable with the unemployment rate having hovered around 4.3 percent
  since May 2025, somewhat above my estimate of the rate consistent
  with maximum employment." Low hire, low fire environment.
  → "Somewhat above" full employment — slightly more dovish on labor
    than Hammack ("near full employment") or Logan ("stable").
    He still sees modest labor market slack.

Benign scenario: "such a meaningful inflation shock could put downward
  pressure on spending... potentially pressuring the U.S. labor market."
  → Acknowledges demand destruction risk from energy shock.
Direction: neutral — modest labor slack acknowledged. [Inference:
  he dissents to remove the easing bias and explicitly accepts labor
  weakness in the adverse scenario — implying inflation currently
  dominates his labor concern — but Kashkari does not state this
  subordination directly.]

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pre-conflict view: Was "fairly confident that core inflation was headed
  back to our 2% target." Tariffs one-time, housing inflation falling,
  nonhousing services tied to cooling wages, long-run expectations
  "well anchored." → He was broadly dovish on inflation pre-conflict.

Post-conflict shift: Oil price increase from Iran conflict "already as
  large as or, by some measures, larger than the increase that resulted
  from the Ukraine war."

BENIGN SCENARIO (Hormuz reopens quickly):
  Oil futures expect prices to fall to ~$88 by year-end. Even in this
  scenario: "Blue Chip forecasters expect core inflation (PCE) to be
  3 percent this year... core inflation will have been at roughly
  3 percent for three years in a row."

ADVERSE SCENARIO (extended Hormuz closure):
  "The price shock wave could be much larger than is currently expected,
   driving up both inflation and unemployment... With inflation having
   been elevated for almost six years and counting, I believe the FOMC
   would have to take very seriously the risk of an unanchoring of
   long-run inflation expectations."
  → Duration framing: "almost six years." Even the benign scenario
    leaves core at 3% for three consecutive years.

Direction: HAWKISH — energy shock as large as Ukraine; three-year 3%
  core in benign scenario; unanchoring risk in adverse.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MONETARY POLICY — EXPLICIT SCENARIO ANALYSIS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BENIGN SCENARIO (Hormuz reopens):
  "I could imagine the optimal monetary policy response to be holding
   rates where they are for an extended period and then easing only
   gradually, once the inflation shock has begun fading, having proven
   to be transitory."
  → Hold for extended period, then gradual cuts. Not an imminent cut
    signal even in the favorable scenario.

ADVERSE SCENARIO (extended closure):
  "Federal funds rate increases, potentially a series of them, could
   be warranted, even at the risk of further weakness to the labor
   market. I firmly believe that anchored long-run inflation
   expectations are necessary for achieving maximum employment."
  → EXPLICIT HIKE SIGNAL in adverse scenario. Willingness to hike
    even with labor market weakness. This is the most hawkish
    statement any voter has made in 2026 — going beyond Waller's
    Apr 17 "hold even if labor weakens" to "hike even if labor weakens."

Dissent rationale:
  "I believe the FOMC should offer a policy outlook that signals that
   the next rate change could be either a cut or a hike, depending on
   how the economy evolves. This could tighten financial conditions
   somewhat today, pushing back against a high-inflation scenario that
   could require an even stronger monetary policy response in the future."
  → Pre-emptive tightening of financial conditions via guidance removal.
    The logic: removing the easing bias now avoids having to hike later.

Cut signal status: ABSENT — extended hold in benign; hikes in adverse.
Direction: HAWKISH — most hawkish voter statement of 2026. Explicit
  hike signal goes beyond any prior voter.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    HAWKISH — most analytical and most hawkish of the three
               dissent statements; explicit hike scenario in adverse case.

Directional:   Kashkari was the most dovish of the three dissenters
  pre-conflict (one SEP cut projected, confident on disinflation).
  The Iran conflict has pushed him to the most explicit hawkish
  position of any voter. His scenario analysis mirrors Waller's
  Apr 17 framework but goes further — Waller said "hold even if
  labor weakens"; Kashkari says "hike even if labor weakens" in
  the adverse scenario.

Language watch:
  • "Core inflation at roughly 3% for three years in a row" —
    the duration framing in the BENIGN scenario. Even the
    good outcome is bad for the look-through case.
  • "Series of hikes... even at the risk of further weakness to
    the labor market" — most hawkish voter statement of 2026.
    If this framing spreads to Hammack/Logan or reaches the Troika,
    the committee's reaction function has fundamentally shifted.
  • "Pre-emptive tightening of financial conditions" via guidance —
    frames the easing bias removal as active tightening, not merely
    a neutral stance. This is a more aggressive intellectual framing
    than Logan's or Hammack's.

Analyst flag: YES
  → Kashkari was constructive on cuts as recently as his March SEP
    (one cut projected). The Iran conflict moved him from a projected
    cutter to an explicit hike advocate in the adverse scenario.
    Analyst to assess: is this a durable shift or is Kashkari reacting
    to the acute uncertainty? His pre-conflict baseline was explicitly
    dovish — tracking whether he reverts if the energy shock fades
    is the key variable.
