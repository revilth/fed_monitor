LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Job gains have remained low." [Mar 18 stmt]
  → Goolsbee: "If you look at the unemployment rate, it hasn't
    gone up much...I prefer looking at rates like the unemployment
    rate, the layoff rate, the hiring rate, the vacancy rate. Most
    of those have shown stability."

Own verdict — KEY:
  "At the moment I think the inflation's got to be a little ahead
   of the employment."
  → Explicit asymmetric risk statement: inflation takes priority
    over labor in his current framework. This is the most direct
    statement of inflation-first from any voter since the energy
    shock began. Goes beyond "dual risk, well positioned."

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Mar 7):   HAWKISH — Mar 7 was uncertain;
  Mar 23 explicitly ranks inflation above labor.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed: none directly.

Own language — KEY:
  "We stopped making progress on the inflation front and at best
   it stalled out and started inching its way back up. So we've
   been waiting for that to have proved transitory to have gone
   away and to pile a new inflation shock before on top of this
   before that one has left the scene. This is going to be a
   tough moment."
  → Cumulative shocks framing: old inflation not yet gone +
    new energy shock = compounding problem. Consistent with his
    Mar 7 "stagflation" naming.

  "The price of gasoline specifically has a very high impact on
   individuals' expectations of what inflation is going to be
   over the next year."
  → Gasoline → expectations transmission is his specific concern.
    Not in other officials' framing at this level of specificity.

  "If we start to see that drifting into inflation expectations,
   you would see more of this rising."
  → Expectations de-anchoring as the key risk he is watching.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Mar 7):   HAWKISH — Mar 7 flagged stagflation
  risk; Mar 23 adds the gasoline-expectations transmission and the
  cumulative compounding shock framing.

MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dissent disclosure (important):
  "Last year I was a voter and I dissented at the last meeting
   of the year [December 2025] because we did not have the data
   to show that inflation was going away."
  → CONFIRMS: Goolsbee voted AGAINST the December 2025 cut.
    He was hawkish before the energy shock — the energy shock
    has reinforced rather than created his current hawkishness.

Own language:
  "I remained fairly optimistic that by the end of 26 rates
   could go down, but I wanted to see proof that we're back on
   an inflation headed to 2%. This definitely throws a wrench
   into the plans."
  → End-2026 cut timeline now under threat from energy shock.

  "The most important thing is to figure out the through line
   of what is happening."
  → Steady hand, no whipsaw — patience. Consistent with Mar 7.

Cut signal status: CONDITIONAL, DEFERRED — end-2026 possible
  but "a wrench in the plans." Inflation must show progress first.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Mar 7):   UNCHANGED on timeline; HAWKISH
  on risk balance (inflation explicitly > labor).
