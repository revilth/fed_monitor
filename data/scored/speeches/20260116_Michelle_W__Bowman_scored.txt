LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Job gains have remained low, and the unemployment rate
   has shown some signs of stabilization." [Jan 28 stmt]
  → Bowman: "unemployment rate...4.4 percent in December...
    increased substantially...reflecting a decline in hiring
    rather than a sharp increase in layoffs."

Own verdict — KEY:
  "The labor market has become more fragile."
  "My focus today is why that fragility poses the greater risk
   and what that means for the path of policy."
  → HAWKISH FRAMING ON LABOR: she is not calling for cuts
    because labor is weak per se — she is calling for cuts
    because labor fragility could deteriorate rapidly.
    This is a preemptive/forward-looking dovish argument.

Own addition — important:
  "Private job gains averaged only about 30,000 per month in
   the fourth quarter...labor demand has continued to gradually
   soften."
  "The share of long-term unemployed workers reached 26 percent
   in December, the highest since early 2022."
  → Points to a low-hire, low-fire market at risk of tipping
    to layoffs. More pessimistic than the committee consensus.

INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation remains somewhat elevated." [Dec 2025 stmt]
  → Bowman: "core PCE inflation...2.9 percent in December.
    However, after removing estimated tariff effects, core PCE
    inflation would have hovered close to 2 percent."

Own language — KEY:
  "The underlying trend in core PCE inflation appears to be
   moving much closer to our 2 percent target than is currently
   showing in the data."
  "Core services inflation is already roughly consistent with
   our target."
  → Most dovish inflation read of any voter: she is essentially
    arguing that ex-tariffs, inflation is already at target.

  "I increasingly confident that inflation will come down
   toward 2 percent as tariff effects on goods inflation
   continue to wane in coming months."
  → Explicit confidence in disinflation resuming. No hedging.

MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Maintain...federal funds rate" → Bowman: "the policy rate
   now reflects a total of 75 basis points of cuts since
   September and is closer to my estimate of its neutral level."

Own language — KEY:
  "I see policy as moderately restrictive."
  "We should remain ready to adjust policy to bring it closer
   to neutral."
  "My approach remains intentionally proactive and forward
   looking...acting in a timely and measured way."
  → Most explicit cut-leaning language of any voter in 2026.
    "Moderately restrictive" (not "well positioned") is her
    frame. "Proactive" cuts before data fully deteriorates.

Cut signal status: PRESENT — "proactive" and "forward looking"
  stance with labor fragility as the trigger, not hard data.

SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    DOVISH — most explicitly dovish voter speech in 2026

Directional:   Bowman is the committee's most pro-cut voter.
  Her frame: ex-tariffs inflation is near target, labor is
  fragile and could deteriorate quickly, and rates are still
  "moderately restrictive." All three arguments point to cuts.
  Her post-FOMC appearances are regulatory Type B — her
  economic outlook view has not been updated since this speech.

Language watch:
  • "Moderately restrictive" — her personal rate characterization;
    contrast with committee's "well positioned." If other
    voters adopt this language, it signals growing cut pressure.
  • "Proactive" cuts — her distinctive framing; not echoed
    by Jefferson or Williams.

Analyst flag: YES
  → Bowman has not given an economic outlook speech since Jan 16.
    The energy shock (late Feb) may have changed her calculus,
    but we have no post-shock data from her. Seek Q&A or
    media appearances for any updated signal.
