FOMC VOTE EXPLANATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"My colleagues and I voted to hold the federal funds rate target
 range at 3-1/2 to 3-3/4 percent."
Voted to hold, not cut. Her reasoning:
  (1) "Some signs of stabilization" in labor → merits a pause.
  (2) Data quality impaired by government shutdown — "reluctant
      to take meaningful signal from the latest data."
  (3) By the March meeting, two more inflation and employment
      reports will be available.
  (4) Inflation "remains somewhat elevated" — adds a slight hawkish
      counterweight to her dovish labor framing.

Counter-factual (what she considered): "I could have voted in favor
  of continuing to remove policy restraint in order to hedge more
  against the risk of further labor market deterioration." —
  She explicitly considered cutting and chose not to.

SEP disclosure: "My SEP includes three cuts for this year." —
  CRITICAL: The most dovish Just Voter's own projection is 3 cuts
  in 2026. This is the baseline against which her subsequent
  speeches should be measured. [ANALYST NOTE: This is a board
  governor disclosing her own SEP dot, which is unusual in its
  specificity.]

GROWTH

  → Mixed: Q3 strong, Q4 pulling back. Consistent with consumption

Direction: NEUTRAL — acknowledges deceleration but not alarmed.

LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Own verdict:
  "The labor market has become increasingly fragile over the past
   year and could continue to deteriorate in the near term." —
  MOST DOVISH LABOR FRAMING of any voter in the early 2026 corpus.
  "Fragile" is more alarming than Jefferson's "downside risks have
  risen" or Williams' "cooling but not deteriorating."

Key data points:
  "Private payroll employment growth slowed further to about 30,000
   per month in Q4." — Extremely weak; well below breakeven.
  "Conference Board job availability index dropped sharply in January
   to its lowest value since early 2021." — Leading indicator of
   further weakness.
  "We could see layoffs rise quickly if firms begin to reassess
   their staffing needs in response to weaker activity." —
   Non-linear risk of rapid deterioration from a fragile state.
  "News of significant additional layoffs in January, as we heard
   this week from two large employers." — Timely data signal.

Own qualifier / addition:
  "History tells us that the labor market can appear to be stable
   right up until it isn't." — Systemic fragility argument; warning
   against complacency from stable initial claims data.

Direction: DOVISH — most dovish labor market characterization
  of any voter in early 2026.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC: [Jan 16 not scored; self-reported "no change"]

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Own language — KEY:
  "After removing these [tariff] effects, core PCE inflation would
   have hovered close to 2 percent in recent months." — Most
   explicit use of tariff-adjusted inflation measure: underlying
   inflation IS at target.
  "12-month core PCE likely stood at or a little below 3 percent
   in December." — Acknowledges elevated headline.
  "The discrepancy... reflects increased volatility in the recent
   data, with unusually large price increases in small categories."
   — Attributes headline elevation to measurement noise.
  "I am confident that inflation will come down toward 2 percent
   as tariff effects on goods inflation continue to wane." —
   HIGH CONFIDENCE forward statement; no qualification.

Direction: DOVISH — tariff-adjusted inflation at target already;
  confidence in disinflation path.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC: [Jan 16 not scored; self-reported "no change"]

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Own language:
  "I continue to see policy as moderately restrictive." —
  DOVISH relative to Troika ("at/near neutral") and consistent
  with Paulson ("still a little restrictive"). Bowman's neutral
  rate estimate is above the current rate.
  "Absent a clear and sustained improvement in labor market
   conditions, we should be ready to adjust policy to bring it
   closer to neutral." — ACTIVE CUT ADVOCACY. The bar for action
   is absence of labor improvement, not a positive cut trigger.
   Much lower cut threshold than Troika language.
  "We should not imply that we expect to maintain the current
   stance of policy for an extended period of time." — Pushes
   back against any "higher for longer" narrative.
  "We should not immediately react if we see inflation go up in
   January." — Pre-emptive dovish guidance on Q1 volatility.
  "We should not rely on [Q1] data as a reason to delay policy
   action if we see a sudden and significant deterioration in
   labor market conditions." — Asymmetric reaction function:
   do not use January data to delay cuts, but act if labor worsens.

Cut signal status: ACTIVE — both a conditional cut signal
  ("absent improvement, ready to adjust") and a SEP projection
  of 3 cuts in 2026.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Jan 16): [Not scored; Bowman self-reports
  no change in views]

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    DOVISH — most dovish Just Voter speech of early 2026.
               Bowman's combination of: (1) "fragile" labor market
               characterization, (2) tariff-adjusted inflation at
               target already, (3) "moderately restrictive" rate
               assessment, (4) SEP projecting 3 cuts, and (5) active
               cut advocacy framing makes her the clearest cut advocate
               among voters in January 2026. Her key risk framing:
               labor deterioration is the primary risk, not inflation.

Language watch:
  • "Fragile" labor — unique and alarming word; no Troika member
    uses this word. If this spreads to Jefferson or Williams, it
    signals a major dovish shift.
  • "Moderately restrictive" (vs. Troika's "at/near neutral") —
    Bowman sees more restriction to remove than the Troika;
    her neutral rate estimate is higher.
  • "Three cuts in 2026" — explicit SEP dot disclosure; the most
    specific forward guidance any voter has given in 2026.
  • "Absent improvement... ready to adjust" — her cut trigger is
    absence of labor improvement (passive), not active labor
    deterioration. Very low bar for cuts.

Analyst flag:  YES
  → Bowman disclosed her SEP: 3 cuts in 2026. This is the most
    specific forward projection from any voter. Post-Iran conflict
    (from May 1, 2026), her SEP projection is almost certainly
    inoperative — the hawkish dissenters' scenario framing implies
    the 3-cut path has been abandoned. Analyst to assess: has Bowman
    updated her views in any subsequent speech, or is she still
    effectively silent (no economic outlook speech post-Jan 30)?
    Her May 1/5/8 speeches are all regulatory Type B.
