LABOR MARKET

  Dec 2025: "labor market conditions have continued to cool"
  → Williams: "Although the labor market cooled over the past couple

INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  Dec 2025: "Inflation has eased over the past two years but remains
  somewhat elevated"
  → Williams echoes this via the data recap: current rate "about
    2-3/4 percent," still above 2%.

Own language — KEY:
  "My current estimate is that the increase in tariffs to date has
   contributed around one half of a percentage point to the current
   inflation rate." — Quantified tariff attribution. Implies ~2.25%
   underlying inflation ex-tariffs.
  "Underlying inflation trends have been pretty favorable, and we're
   seeing no signs of broader inflationary pressures." — Dovish on
   underlying; tariff-induced, not demand-driven.
  "Inflation will peak at around 2-3/4 to 3 percent sometime during
   the first half of this year, before starting to fall back." —
   Explicit peak forecast: H1 2026.
  "Inflation will be just under 2-1/2 percent for this year as a
   whole, before reaching the FOMC's longer-run 2 percent goal in
   2027." — Specific 2-year disinflation path.

  Tariffs: "Largely one-off effect on prices that will be fully
   realized this year." — Look-through framing; transitory pass-through.

SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    NEUTRAL / BASELINE — establishes Williams' January 2026
               stance. The key framing: tariffs are transitory/one-off,
               underlying inflation is benign, labor is cooling but not
               deteriorating, and rates are "closer to neutral" (not yet
               at neutral). No explicit cut signal. The optimistic growth
               outlook (2.5–2.75%) and specific disinflation path
               (2% by 2027) set a constructive baseline.

Language watch:
  • "Closer to neutral" (not AT neutral) — Troika-level signal that
    rate cuts are not yet justified on a neutral-rate basis; subsequent
    speeches should track if this language shifts to "at neutral" or
    "above neutral."
  • "Largely one-off effect" on tariff inflation — look-through framing;
    watch for this to erode if core inflation stays elevated.
  • "Stabilize this year then gradually come down" on unemployment —
    constructive labor framing; watch if weakened in subsequent speeches.
