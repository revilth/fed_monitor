GROWTH

"Activity in the US economy has been resilient thus far in 2026."

Direction: neutral.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "unemployment rate has been little changed" [Apr 29 stmt]
  → Hammack: "The unemployment rate has been little changed near
    my estimate of full employment since last summer."

Own verdict: "Near my estimate of full employment" — she explicitly
  places the current unemployment rate at or near full employment.
  This is the most hawkish labor characterization from any voter:
  no downside risk, no softening, no "susceptible to shocks."
  Jefferson (Apr 7): "susceptible to adverse shocks." Powell (Apr 29):
  "labor demand has clearly softened." Hammack sees neither concern.

Direction: HAWKISH — "near full employment" is the most restrictive
  labor characterization of any voter. [Inference: by placing labor
  at full employment, Hammack removes the dovish leg of the dual-risk
  argument — she does not state this framing directly.]

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation is elevated, in part reflecting the recent increase in
   global energy prices." [Apr 29 stmt]
  → Hammack does NOT echo the energy attribution framing.

Own language — KEY:
  "Inflation pressures continue to be broad based, and rising oil
   prices present an additional source of inflationary pressure."
  → IMPORTANT FRAMING DIFFERENCE. The statement attributes elevated
    inflation "in part" to energy — a dovish framing that isolates
    energy as the cause. Hammack frames inflation as "broad based"
    and treats energy as an ADDITIONAL pressure ON TOP of existing
    broad inflation. This is considerably more hawkish than the
    statement's attribution framework.

Direction: HAWKISH — rejects the statement's energy-attribution
  framing; describes inflation as broad-based with energy as additive.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dissent rationale (full):
  "I supported holding the federal funds rate steady. I dissented
   from the post-meeting statement because I did not believe it was
   appropriate to include an easing bias around the future path for
   monetary policy. The current FOMC statement references language
   around 'additional adjustments.' This forward guidance was put
   into the statement to signal a pause rather than an end to the
   easing cycle. I see this clear easing bias as no longer appropriate
   given the outlook."

  → Hammack is not ambiguous: she wants the easing bias removed now.
    Her reasoning: the language was designed to signal a pause (not
    a terminal hold), but the current outlook — broad inflation, full
    employment, energy as additive pressure — no longer warrants even
    that implicit cut signal.

Risk assessment:
  "Uncertainty around the economic outlook is elevated, with upside
   risks to inflation and downside risks to growth and employment."
  → She acknowledges downside growth/employment risks — she is not
    calling for a hike. The symmetric risk framing explains why she
    supports a hold. [Inference: her "broad based" inflation framing
    combined with "near full employment" labor effectively removes both
    sides of a dovish argument — she does not state this combined
    implication explicitly.]

Cut signal status: EXPLICITLY OPPOSED — wants easing bias removed.
Direction: HAWKISH — most hawkish voter statement since Logan Feb 10.

SUMMARY

Net signal:    HAWKISH — first post-FOMC statement from any voter;
               most hawkish economic characterization from any

  • "Near my estimate of full employment" — [Inference: places labor
