GROWTH
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  Dec 2025: "economic activity has continued to expand at a solid pace"
  → Barkin: "U.S. economy has proven remarkably resilient";
    Q3 GDP 4.3%. Consistent with statement framing.

Own language:
  "Demand is narrow. The two engines of today's economy are the
   AI ecosystem and wealthy consumers." — COMPOSITIONAL WARNING:
   Barkin is the first in the 2026 corpus to explicitly flag demand
   narrowness as a tail risk. This goes beyond the committee's
   statement language.
  "What would happen if the AI frenzy were to ease? It has been
   supporting virtually all of the growth in business investment."
   — Single-sector dependency risk named explicitly.
  Optimistic counterweight: "A lot of stimulus is set to come into
   the economy... fiscal stimulus from the recent tax bill... gasoline
   prices are down... deregulatory initiatives... rate cuts."
   — Lists six tailwinds. Balanced framing.

LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  Dec 2025: "labor market conditions have continued to cool"
  → Barkin echoes via data: unemployment 4.6%, job growth narrow.

Own verdict:
  "Job growth seems narrow as well. Through November, the economy
   added an average of 70,000 private sector jobs per month in 2025.
   But health care and social assistance alone comprised 63,000
   of those." — 90% of job growth in one sector. Most concentrated
   characterization of labor in early 2026 Troika/voter speeches.
  "Not hiring but not firing; Not cutting back on investments but
   not leaning into more growth." — Uncertainty-driven stasis.
   Vivid on-the-ground description of business behavior.

Own qualifier / addition:
  "Both sides of our mandate bear watching. Unemployment remains
   low on a historic basis but has ticked up." — Balanced dual-mandate
   framing. Does NOT express elevated downside risk (contrast with
   Jefferson: "downside risks to employment have risen").

INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  Dec 2025: "inflation has eased... but remains somewhat elevated"
  → Barkin: "inflation has come down but remains above target."
  → Minimal echo; he focuses more on the personal addition below.

Own language — KEY:
  "No one wants higher inflation expectations to get embedded."
  — Duration concern expressed indirectly through the negative
    framing ("no one wants"). Hawkish lean.
  "Inflation above target now for almost five years." — Duration
   framing. Same "five years" language that Logan will use in her
   May 1 dissent statement, months before her. Barkin introduces
   this duration argument in January. [Analyst note: watch whether
   this specific duration framing spreads.]
  Core PCE at 2.8% — cited without characterizing it as near-target.
  Consumers "have pushed back" on price increases: "trading down to
   lower-priced retailers and private labels." — Businesses facing
   demand-side pushback; inflationary pass-through is muted by
   consumer resistance. Slightly dovish on future inflation.

SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    NEUTRAL with hawkish lean — The "almost five years
               above target" duration framing and demand-narrowness
               compositional warning tilt Barkin slightly hawkish
               relative to the committee's baseline. He offers more
               risk texture than the Troika speeches (Williams/
               Jefferson) but reaches the same "at/near neutral,
               data-dependent" policy conclusion.

Language watch:
  • "Almost five years above target" — duration framing; first use
    in 2026 corpus; later appears in Logan's May 1 dissent statement.
    If this spreads to the Troika, it raises the bar for cuts.
  • "Demand is narrow" — compositional risk language; more specific
    than committee statement language. Barkin uses this regularly —
    track whether it spreads to voters.
  • "Within the range of neutral" — Barkin's rate characterization
    is more explicitly uncertain than Jefferson's "at neutral";
    he preserves optionality in both directions.

Analyst flag:  NO — Non-voter; flags worth tracking but no immediate
  action. Note the early appearance of the duration framing.
