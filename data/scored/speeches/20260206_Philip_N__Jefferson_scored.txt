"Economic Outlook and Supply-Side (Dis)Inflation Dynamics"

The economic outlook and policy sections are fully Type A;

Reference FOMC:    January 28, 2026
Pre-FOMC baseline: Jan 16, 2026  "Economic Outlook and Monetary Policy Implementation"
Post-FOMC prior:   N/A (first post-Jan28 speech)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

LABOR MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Job gains have remained low, and the unemployment rate has
   shown some signs of stabilization." [Jan 28 stmt]
  → Jefferson: "unemployment rate was 4.4 percent in December
    and has changed little in recent months...pace of job
    creation has eased."

Own verdict:
  "I see the overall labor market as roughly in balance, with
   a low-hiring, low-firing environment prevailing."
  → Neutral. Stable characterization, no directional lean.

Own qualifier:
  "In this less dynamic labor market, the downside risks to
   employment remain, but my baseline is for the unemployment
   rate to hold approximately steady throughout this year."
  → Acknowledges downside risks but baseline is stability.
    Less pessimistic than Bowman on fragility.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Jan 16): MINIMAL — same "roughly in
  balance" characterization. Jan 16 noted slowdown; Feb 6
  confirms stabilization. Slight dovish relief.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INFLATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  "Inflation remains somewhat elevated." [Jan 28 stmt]
  → Jefferson: "inflation remains elevated relative to our
    2 percent target...progress on disinflation has stalled
    over the past year."

Own language — KEY:
  "The stall in the disinflationary process is mainly because
   of tariffs...I expect the disinflationary process to resume
   this year once increased tariffs pass through more fully
   to prices."
  → Dovish anchor: tariff inflation is temporary; disinflation
    resumes when pass-through completes.

  "A one-time shift in the price level...anchored inflation
   expectations should limit second-round effects."
  → Explicitly adopted the committee's key dovish framing.
    No second-round effects = no need to tighten further.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Jan 16): UNCHANGED — same "tariffs are
  temporary, disinflation resumes" framework. Very stable.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MONETARY POLICY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOMC statement language echoed:
  → Jefferson: "I supported the FOMC's decision last week to
    maintain the federal funds rate at the current level."

Own language:
  "Those adjustments put our policy rate broadly in the range
   of neutral while maintaining a balanced approach."
  "The current policy stance is well positioned to address
   the risks to both sides of our dual mandate."
  → Explicit neutral-rate characterization: rates are in the
    neutral range (not restrictive). This is the key signal —
    it sets a higher bar for further cuts since he is not
    arguing rates are restricting the economy.

Cut signal status: ABSENT — "well positioned" holding formula,
  no directional guidance.

Shift vs. pre-FOMC (Jan 16): MARGINAL — Jan 16 said "well
  positioned to determine extent and timing of additional
  adjustments," implying future action possible. Feb 6 drops
  this framing and emphasizes current neutral positioning.
  Slight hawkish lean on monetary policy section.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Net signal:    NEUTRAL — stable hold, broadly in neutral range

Directional:   Jefferson's Feb 6 speech establishes his
  baseline: labor is stabilizing, tariff inflation is
  temporary, rates are broadly neutral. The "well positioned"
  formula replaces any forward guidance. This is the most
  stable, committee-consensus-oriented speech of any Troika
  member in early 2026.

Language watch:
  • "Policy rate broadly in the range of neutral" — this is
    a significant admission if adopted by others; it reduces
    the mechanical case for cuts. Williams does NOT use this
    language in Mar 3. Track if Jefferson upgrades or
    downgrades this characterization.
  • "One-time shift in the price level / no second-round
    effects" — confirming this as committee-wide language.

Analyst flag: NO — trajectory stable and clear.
