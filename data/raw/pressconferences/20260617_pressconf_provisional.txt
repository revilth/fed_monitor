  3. Data ("use and reliance on existing data streams")
  4. Productivity and jobs
  5. Causes of inflation

Task force charge (verbatim):

Q&A (unscripted — Chair's personal signals)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: [On reconsidering the 2% inflation target]
A: "That is the Federal Reserve's long-held objective of 2%. The 'two' is the
   left of the decimal point. For now, 'zero' is to the right."

Q: [On the dot plot / SEP submission]
A: "It's been the practice of this committee for participants to submit these
   projections, and I have encouraged my colleagues to continue to do so.
   I, however, have refrained from offering any projections of my own,
   consistent with my long-held views on the SEP, at least as currently
   structured."
   "I did not submit a dot for me. It's not helpful in the conduct of policy."

Q: [On what the dots with hike projections signal]
A: "Despite the recent pullback in oil, half of the members of the FOMC expect
   rate hikes as soon as this year, reflecting strong labor market and inflation
   data."

Q: [On the new statement design]
A: The statement is "a bit shorter, a bit simpler" and removes forward guidance
   as "not well suited to the current policy conjuncture."
