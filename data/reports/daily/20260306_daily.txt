1.  COLLINS  |  Boston Fed President  |  Non Voter
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  2025 real GDP growth stronger than expected. For 2026 baseline:
  "continued solid growth." Collins' regional intelligence: businesses
  report rising costs for insurance, energy, construction — margin
  compression reducing capital investment. Aggregate solid; ground-
  level more cautious.
  Direction: neutral — solid with regional cost-pressure caveat.

LABOR MARKET
  "Relatively balanced labor market conditions." Her baseline for
  2026 features continued balance. No fragility language. Neutral
  read consistent with committee consensus.
  Direction: neutral.

INFLATION
  "My outlook has demand exerting some upward pressure on prices,
  slowing the return of inflation to 2 percent." "Mildly restrictive"
  rates. Key statement: "I do not see an urgency for additional policy
  adjustments, and I will be looking for clear evidence that inflation
  is moving durably toward the 2 percent target — something that might
  occur only over the second half of the year."
  → H2 2026 is her evidence timeline for disinflation confirmation.
  Direction: neutral-patient — demand still adding some pressure;
  H2 evidence timeline.

MONETARY POLICY
  "Maintain policy rates at their current, mildly restrictive levels
  for some time." Patient, no urgency, H2 evidence required.
  Direction: neutral — patience; no cut signal.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL-PATIENT — "mildly restrictive"; H2 evidence
  timeline; no urgency

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (N/A — baseline): Established.
vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (N/A): N/A
vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:  Middle of the Non Voter spectrum. More
  patient than Bowman (Just Voter); "Mildly restrictive" sits between
  Bowman's "moderately restrictive" and Logan's "near neutral." Logan

2.  GOOLSBEE  |  Chicago Fed President  |  Non Voter
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH / LABOR MARKET
  Jobs report day. February payrolls: "tough miss." Unemployment
  ticking up — "one month is not a trend, but it's not a good month."
  He rejects using total payrolls as the key indicator (immigration
  effects on break-even). Rate-based measures still showing steadiness.
  Direction: neutral — labor not deteriorating, one bad print doesn't
  change his view.

INFLATION
  "The inflation hasn't been ideal. Has at least stalled out at kind
  of 3% and some of the latest measures the inflation disturbingly
  high in non-tariff categories like services." On the oil price shock:
  "It can lead you in a stagflationary direction... That's always the
  worst case scenario for the central bank because there's not an
  obvious monetary policy answer."
  → FIRST explicit stagflation naming by any official in 2026.
  "Each time we add an uncertainty, I kind of think the time at
  which it makes sense to act keeps getting pushed back."
  Direction: hawkish — stagflation named; services inflation
  "disturbingly high"; cut timeline deferred further.

MONETARY POLICY
  "There's a time for sniffing and a time for walking. When there's
  uncertainty and you're getting conflicting data points, that's the
  time for sniffing." Pre-FOMC holding stance; no commitment before
  seeing more data.
  Direction: neutral — wait-and-see; no action imminent.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL-HAWKISH — stagflation first named; services
  inflation concern deepens; timeline pushed back
