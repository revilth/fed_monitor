⚠ KEY FINDING: Goolsbee is materially more hawkish than his
public reputation. This date represents his clearest 2026
policy articulation. Do not rely on the "Goolsbee is dovish"
heuristic.

GROWTH
  Consumer spending is "the main driver of growth" — broad-based,
  not just high-income households. AI investment is a secondary
  factor; its direct GDP contribution is smaller than often portrayed
  once imports are netted out. Growth backdrop is solid; not a
  policy concern for him.
  Direction: neutral.

LABOR MARKET
  "The job market has been steady." Unemployment 4.3%, essentially
  flat for a year. Low hiring but also low firing — unemployment
  stable near break-even. He rejects the recession narrative: low
  aggregate payrolls reflect immigration-driven lower break-even,
  not cyclical weakness. Rate-based measures (unemployment, layoff
  rate, vacancy rate) show steadiness at levels "comparable to
  normal expansions." The one weak signal: hiring rate is low — "a
  warning flag" but "relatively steady for a year and a half."
  Direction: neutral — stable read; rejects downside alarm.

INFLATION
  Core PCE ~3%. Progress stalled in 2025. "It is not obvious that
  our interest rate policy is even restrictive. The real federal
  funds rate measured that way is pretty close or even a bit below
  the consensus view of long-run r*." [NABE] Core services ex-housing
  at 3.3%: "It's harder to make an optimistic case that high services
  inflation is just transitory. So we need to be vigilant." "Since
  we have been burned by assuming transitory inflation before...we
  should be careful not to put ourselves in a difficult position."
  → Explicitly invokes the 2021-22 lesson. Raises vigilance bar
  on reflexive "look through."
  Bloomberg interview adds: describes the inflation picture as
  "stalled out" and "inching back up" — concerned tone.
  Direction: hawkish — explicitly questions whether rates are
  restrictive; "vigilant" on services inflation.

MONETARY POLICY
  "I feel that front-loading too many rate cuts is not prudent."
  "Before we cut rates more, let's be sure inflation is heading
  back to 2%." [NABE] Bloomberg: "The time at which it makes sense
  to act keeps getting pushed back" with each new uncertainty.
  Open to cuts later in 2026 if inflation resumes, but sets an
  explicit evidence bar first.
  Direction: neutral-hawkish — conditional cuts; explicit evidence
  bar; not prudent to front-load.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL-HAWKISH — most important hawkish finding
  from any Non Voter to date; "not obvious rates are restrictive"
  joins Logan's Feb 10 near-neutral framing (Logan is a Just Voter)
