━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  "The single strongest driver of growth of the US economy has been
  the relentless uptick of consumer spending. Just regular consumers
  with decent wage growth spending."
  Consumer spending is characterized as broad-based and sustained.
  Downside risk: precautionary saving from consumer sentiment
  deterioration. Tax refund data point: 50% of refunds going into
  savings, "higher than normal perhaps. And that wouldn't be a great
  sign."
  Direction: NEUTRAL — growth is resilient but flagged as fragile
  if consumer sentiment continues to deteriorate.

LABOR MARKET
  "Stable without being good."
  Four horsemen framework: unemployment rate, vacancy rate, hiring
  rate, layoff rate — all stable for ~18 months but sending
  conflicting signals. Hiring rate = recession-depth lows; layoff
  rate = boom-peak lows simultaneously. His interpretation: firms in
  wait-and-see mode due to uncertainty ("we just want this to be
  resolved in the next six months"), not deteriorating conditions.
  Direction: NEUTRAL — characterized as stable but not improving.

INFLATION
  Reacting to morning CPI: "Not only are we not making progress,
  it's going the wrong way. And not just in oil related things and
  not just in tariff related things. Now you've seen drifting upward
  of services inflation. And I keep highlighting for me that's the
  part that I'm nervous about. I want to see that at least stop
  growing and hopefully start going back down because that can't
  really be from oil prices."

  NEW SIGNAL: Services inflation drifting up is the specific concern.
  This component cannot be explained by oil or tariffs — it is a
  domestic demand / structural signal and the hardest to look through.

  "The unexpectedly disappointing part in my mind was on the services
  side" — this was real-time new information even for him; he did not
  anticipate the services strength in the morning print.

  Energy: look-through endorsed conditionally ("if the Middle East
  conflict proves to be passing, that should come down just as fast
  as it went up"). The IF is load-bearing.
  Direction: HAWKISH LEAN — services inflation drifting up in
  unexpected fashion is a new and harder-to-dismiss concern.

MONETARY POLICY
  "I remain optimistic and I was before the inflation started going up
  that rates can come down still a fair amount but we've got to see
  progress on inflation and if it were up to me we would kind of adopt
  that mindset."

  — "Rates can come down still a fair amount": cutting path is
    acknowledged as real and meaningful.
  — "We've got to see progress on inflation": services inflation
    drifting up is the primary blockage.
  — "If it were up to me": reminder he is a Non Voter in 2026.

  Cut signal: CONDITIONAL — path available but blocked by services
  inflation. The signal is LESS HAWKISH than Mar 23's "a wrench in
  the plans" — implying the cutting path has survived despite the
  shock sequence.
  Direction: NEUTRAL-TO-DOVISH on the cut path; HAWKISH on the
  specific inflation signal that has to clear first.

NET SIGNAL: MIXED — hawkish on inflation (services concern is new
  and hard to look through), dovish lean on cut signal (path remains
  available), neutral on labor (drops Mar 23's explicit inflation-
  over-labor ranking).

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (May 8, Hoover):     Hawkish escalation on inflation
  (services specific signal added); no cut signal at Hoover, weak
  conditional cut signal at Rockford.
vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (Mar 23, CNBC):   Labor: DOVISH (Mar 23 ranked
  inflation explicitly over labor; Rockford does not). Inflation:
  HAWKISH (services concern added). Cut signal: DOVISH (Rockford
  reinstates "rates can come down still a fair amount" vs. Mar 23's
  "wrench in plans").
vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:            Goolsbee sits in the middle of
  the post-FOMC committee distribution — more dovish than Logan/
  Kashkari/Hammack on the cut signal, more hawkish than Bowman on
  inflation. His services concern, if shared by Paulson (median voter),
  would shift the committee's effective bar for cuts upward.

LANGUAGE TO WATCH:
  • "Stable without being good" — labor verdict likely to be cited.
    Watch for spread to other officials' characterizations.
  • "Services going the wrong way" — if this becomes Paulson's or
    Jefferson's language, it is a committee-wide signal that the
    hardest inflation component is the new blockage.
  • "Rates can come down still a fair amount" — dovish vs. recent
    framing. Watch for any Troika echo as a signal the cutting path
