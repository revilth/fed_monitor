1.  JEFFERSON  |  Vice Chair  |  Troika
    [First post-FOMC speech; dual-risk formula introduced]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  2025 GDP ~2%; expects similar or slightly faster pace in 2026.
  AI investment, business formation, and deregulation as upside.
  Elevated energy costs could weigh on consumer and business spending
  if sustained — the key downside risk to growth.
  Direction: neutral — solid but energy-dependent.

LABOR MARKET
  Near-verbatim FOMC statement echo: "The unemployment rate has been
  relatively low and little changed in recent months." Own verdict:
  "I see the labor market as roughly in balance." NEW addition:
  "but susceptible to adverse shocks." Also: "risks to my labor
  market forecast are skewed to the downside."
  → First use of both "susceptible to adverse shocks" and "skewed
    to the downside" — important new qualifiers vs. his Feb 6 stance.
  Direction: mild dovish lean — downside risk flags now explicit.

INFLATION
  Maintains "tariffs one-time" thesis. NEW: energy shock complicates
  his prior expectation. "The ongoing trade policy uncertainty and
  geopolitical tensions pose upside risk to my inflation forecast."
  → "Upside risk to my inflation forecast" is new personal language —
    admits his baseline has deteriorated.
  Direction: slight hawkish lean vs. Feb 6.

MONETARY POLICY
  "I confront an outlook where there is downside risk to the labor
  market and upside risk to inflation. I am confident that our
  current policy stance is well positioned to respond to a range
  of outcomes." → Dual-risk formula introduced; "well positioned"
  stable holding formula.
  Direction: unchanged — patient hold.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL — dual-risk formula established; new labor
  downside qualifiers and inflation upside acknowledgment

2.  BARR  |  Governor  |  Just Voter
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  "Economy has continued to grow at a solid pace, supported by
  resilient consumer spending, substantial productivity growth,
  and exceptionally strong business investment in AI." Solid.
  Direction: neutral.

LABOR MARKET
  "Low levels of hiring likely leave the labor market vulnerable
  to shocks." Zero labor force growth + zero job creation = balanced
  but fragile. Vigilance warranted.
  Direction: neutral with fragility flag.

INFLATION
  "We have had five years now of inflation at elevated levels, and
  near-term inflation expectations have risen again, so I am
  particularly concerned that yet another price shock could increase
  longer-term inflation expectations. We need to be especially
  vigilant."
  → "Five years" credibility clock + "rising near-term expectations"
    + "especially vigilant" = strongest explicit inflation vigilance
    language from any Board Governor this cycle.
  Direction: hawkish lean.

MONETARY POLICY
  "It makes sense to take some time to assess conditions." Patient hold.
  Direction: unchanged — hold.

NET SIGNAL: HAWKISH LEAN — credibility clock; expectations concern;
  "especially vigilant"

  will become the committee's stable post-FOMC language.
