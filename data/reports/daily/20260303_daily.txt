GROWTH
  GDP grew at a solid pace in 2025; Williams expects real GDP to
  grow ~2.5% in 2026, supported by fiscal stimulus, favorable
  financial conditions, and AI investment. "The U.S. economy
  appears to be on a good footing." No growth concern.
  Direction: neutral — confident, not a policy driver.

LABOR MARKET
  "In recent months, however, there have been promising signs of
  stabilization." Unemployment 4.3%, back to July 2025 level. Job
  growth and Labor Market Tightness Index have stabilized. The
  low-hire, low-fire market persists; survey-based measures (Conference
  Board, NY Fed) haven't confirmed stabilization — a cautionary note.
  Expects unemployment to edge down over 2026 and 2027.
  Direction: neutral — stabilization with soft-data caveat.

INFLATION
  "No signs of significant second-round effects from tariffs." "No
  global supply-chain bottlenecks. Wage growth stable." Underlying
  inflation ex-imports moving in the right direction. Expects tariffs
  to have "one-off effects on prices" — disinflation resumes H2 2026.
  2026 inflation forecast: ~2.5%. 2027: 2%. His most complete
  articulation of the committee's "look through" doctrine on tariffs.
  Direction: dovish — confident disinflation narrative; no second-
  round effects.

MONETARY POLICY
  ⚠ CUT SIGNAL PRESENT — most significant Troika statement in 2026:
  "If inflation follows the path I expect, further reductions in the
  federal funds rate will eventually be warranted to prevent monetary
  policy from inadvertently becoming more restrictive."
  This is explicit forward guidance on cuts from a Troika member —
  the furthest-reaching policy signal of the year so far.
  Direction: dovish — explicit conditional cut signal.

NET SIGNAL: DOVISH — most explicit Troika cut signal in 2026 to date
