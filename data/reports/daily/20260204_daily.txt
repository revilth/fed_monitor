GROWTH
  GDP rose 4.4% annualized in Q3 2025; full-year growth estimated
  slightly above 2%. Cook flags a "K-shaped" divergence: wealthy
  households (stock-market beneficiaries) spending strongly;
  lower/middle-income households showing rising delinquencies and
  stagnant spending. "I am carefully watching sentiment, delinquencies,
  and other indicators that show a worsening outlook for low- and
  moderate-income households." Strong aggregate, fragile beneath.
  Direction: neutral — solid headline, flagged distributional concern.

LABOR MARKET
  "The labor market is roughly in balance, but I am highly attentive
  to developments, knowing it can shift quickly." Unemployment 4.4%,
  little changed in H2 2025. Layoffs low; but youth and Black
  unemployment have risen, and involuntary part-time work jumped.
  Direction: neutral with downside vigilance.

INFLATION
  "I see risks as tilted toward higher inflation." Core PCE ~3%,
  progress stalled. Cook is explicit: she will not ease until she
  sees sustained evidence of disinflation. "After nearly five years
  of above-target inflation, it is essential that we maintain our
  credibility." Credibility clock framing — prolonged inflation
  raises her hawkish threshold. Anchored expectations are the
  dovish floor, but she is watching the clock.
  Direction: hawkish lean — explicit asymmetric risk tilt.

MONETARY POLICY
  Supported the January 28 hold. "Until I see stronger evidence that
  inflation is moving sustainably back down to target, that is where
  my focus will be, in the absence of unexpected changes in the labor
  market." Inflation-first holding stance; no cut signal.
  Direction: hawkish lean.

NET SIGNAL: HAWKISH LEAN — inflation-first; credibility-clock framing;
  no cut signal
