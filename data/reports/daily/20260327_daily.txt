1.  PAULSON  |  Philadelphia Fed President  |  Just Voter
    [Median voter — most important Just Voter reading]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  Paulson frames growth through three AI/productivity scenarios
  (low, medium, high) with implications for R*. Her current R*
  estimate is ~3.1% (SEP median), but she sees a plausible range.
  Structural growth concern: if AI is transformative, the neutral
  rate rises; if not, it stays lower. She does not provide a
  near-term growth characterization.
  Direction: neutral — structural framing, not cyclical signal.

LABOR MARKET
  "I see no signs that the labor market is contributing to inflation
  today." Labor is not adding price pressure. Near full employment
  complicates patience calculations.
  Direction: neutral.

INFLATION
  "Inflation has been above our 2-percent target for six years.
  While we've made significant progress, inflation is still 2.8
  percent, not 2 percent." KEY STATEMENT: "If inflation were at
  the 2-percent target, I would feel more comfortable being patient.
  But if inflation is above 2 percent and has been for some time,
  I would be more cautious."
  → Paulson's explicit inflation-first bar. At 2.8% PCE, she is
    NOT in the patience zone. More restrictive than Jefferson's
    symmetric dual-risk framing.
  Also: "Long-term inflation expectations are consistent with 2
  percent, but they may also be a little more fragile."
  → Personal qualifier on expectations — more cautious than the
    standard "well anchored."
  Direction: hawkish lean — explicit conditions for patience
  that are not currently met.

MONETARY POLICY
  "We are better able to calibrate monetary policy when we start
  from a good place." Her "good place" = 2% inflation. Since we're
  not there, flexibility is not yet available.
  Direction: hawkish lean — patience conditional on 2% inflation.

NET SIGNAL: HAWKISH LEAN — inflation-first; explicit 2% precondition
  for patience; fragile expectations qualifier

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
2.  BARKIN  |  Richmond Fed President  |  Non Voter
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  GDP grew 2% in 2025. Consumer resilience evident in credit card
  data. Strong earnings, low unemployment, elevated asset values
  support spending. AI investment generating concrete results at
  the firm level. BUT: demand remains "narrow" — concentrated in
  AI ecosystem and wealthy customers. Easing in AI space could
  ripple through business investment and equity wealth effects.
  Direction: neutral — solid with concentration risk.

LABOR MARKET
  Unemployment hovering around 4.4% for 6 months. "Labor market
  has stabilized." But: "Employment growth is near zero, and younger
  workers are having a hard time finding jobs in this low-hire
  economy." Multiple applicants for every position; employers
  reluctant to hire. "The labor market just feels a bit fragile."
  Direction: neutral — stable headline, ground-level fragility.

INFLATION
  "The foggiest part of the outlook is the path forward on inflation."
  On the ground: businesses recognize loss of pricing power —
  more than 9 in 10 firms can't raise prices to desired levels.
  BUT: "The latest in a series of cost-increasing supply shocks
  after the pandemic, semiconductor chip shortages, the war in
  Ukraine, tariffs, and immigration enforcement."
  → "Series of cost-increasing supply shocks" — independent
    articulation of the cumulative-shock framing (Goolsbee Mar 7
    named stagflation; now Barkin names the series explicitly).
    This is the same analytical frame spreading across officials.
  Direction: genuinely uncertain — pricing-power loss (disinflationary)
  vs. series of supply shocks (inflationary). Fog metaphor apt.

MONETARY POLICY
  "We've lowered rates 175 basis points over the last 18 months,
  leaving the fed funds rate at the higher end of the range of
  neutral as defined by FOMC participants and outside analysts."
  → Third official to use near-neutral characterization (Logan Feb 10,
    Goolsbee Feb 24, Barkin Mar 27). Growing dispersed view.
  Direction: neutral — higher end of neutral; fog justifies patience.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL — genuine uncertainty; on-the-ground
  disinflationary signals offset by supply shock concerns
