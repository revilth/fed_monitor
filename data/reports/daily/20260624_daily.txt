Pre-recorded welcome to the Cleveland Fed's State of Small Business Symposium,
marking 10 years of the Federal Reserve's Small Business Credit Survey (SBCS).
Content is entirely about small-business research and the survey's value: the
prevalence of small firms (99.9% of U.S. businesses; 61% of net new jobs since
1995), and one survey finding — "nearly half of small employer firms reported
using AI in some capacity, and 71% of these firms reported increased
productivity." She frames this as evidence that small/new firms are
"resourceful, adaptable" adopters of frontier technology.

MONETARY POLICY CONTENT: None. The only mandate-adjacent sentence is boilerplate
— "Achieving our dual mandate of maximum employment and stable prices will
create the conditions where small businesses and all Americans can thrive." No
growth, labor, inflation, or rate-path characterization. Pure Type B.

NET SIGNAL: N/A — no policy content. Cook's most recent scorable Type A remains
her last economic-outlook appearance; this welcome adds nothing to her stance.

The AI-productivity finding is notable only as a data point alongside the
broader committee theme (echoed by Goolsbee June 25 and in the June 17
statement's "productivity growth … strong" language) — but Cook draws no
policy implication from it here.
