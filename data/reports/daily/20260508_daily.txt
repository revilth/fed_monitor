INFLATION
  Core of the speech: Daly presents an inflation dashboard methodology
  and applies it to the current tariff and oil shock sequence.

  On tariff shock: "More confidence, not complete confidence, that
  looking through was a reasonable strategy to have while you're being
  watchful that something else might change." Dashboard showed mostly
  green for the tariff shock — look-through endorsed with qualification.

  On oil shock addition: "Now we have the oil shock on top of that...
  you're starting to see red in the areas that you'd expect." The
  dashboard is now flashing concern. Supply chain transmission is
  her named tripwire: NY Fed global supply chain index.

  Key hedge: "This doesn't say you're going to have more persistent
  inflation. It says you're going to have more persistent inflation
  pressure." Careful distinction — pressure ≠ realized persistence.

  Scenario framing: DESCRIPTIVE, not explicit. Daly names the supply
  chain mechanism as the persistence channel but does not pre-commit
  to a named policy response.
  Direction: HAWKISH LEAN — tariff look-through endorsed; oil shock
  causing dashboard to flash red; supply chain index as tripwire.

MONETARY POLICY
  No direct cut/hold signal. The policy implication is embedded in the
  dashboard: if the supply chain index stays elevated alongside the oil
  shock, the look-through becomes untenable. The framing is watchful
  and data-dependent without pre-commitment.
  Cut signal: ABSENT
  Direction: HAWKISH LEAN (via dashboard conditions)

NET SIGNAL: HAWKISH LEAN — dashboard flagging orange-to-red on oil
  shock; supply chain index as her named tripwire for abandoning

GROWTH
  Not addressed directly. Embedded framework implies AI data center
  investment concentrated in Silicon Valley is a pull-forward expression
  of expected future productivity — a demand-side overheat risk, not a
  growth concern per se.
  Direction: neutral

LABOR MARKET
  Not addressed as a current outlook topic. Historical GFC case study
  only (natural rate of unemployment debate).
  Direction: neutral

INFLATION
  No direct current-cycle inflation assessment in prepared remarks.
  The analytical framework has inflation implications:
  — Unexpected productivity: inflation falls, rates fall.
  — Expected productivity (AI hype): wealth-effect pull-forward
    overheats economy; central bank must raise nominal rate.
  — Expected boom that disappoints: stagflation risk.
  Applied: "The bigger the share of the total productivity boom that
  is still on the way, the more likely it is that rates are going to
  need to rise." Current AI productivity is predominantly expected,
  not yet delivered.
  Direction: HAWKISH LEAN (via natural rate / r-star implications)

MONETARY POLICY
  "The bigger the hype, the bigger the concern."
  → Compresses the r-star elevation argument into one quotable line.
    If AI productivity is as widely anticipated as surveys suggest
    (1 ppt/year for 10 years), the natural rate is elevated — i.e.,
    current policy is less restrictive than it appears.
  → Consistent with his Feb 24 "not obvious rates are restrictive"
    framing, now with a fully developed theoretical backing.
  Cut signal: ABSENT
  Direction: HAWKISH LEAN

NET SIGNAL: HAWKISH LEAN — AI/natural rate framework implies r-star
  is elevated; "the bigger the hype, the bigger the concern"; cut signal
  absent; complements existing "rates may not be restrictive" framing.

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (Mar 23):    HAWKISH LEAN — adds the r-star elevation
  channel as a new theoretical basis for the existing "not restrictive"
  intuition.
vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (Mar 23): Same.
vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:     Among the most hawkish Non Voter signals.
  The r-star argument, if adopted by the Troika or key voters, would
  imply the neutral rate has moved up — materially reducing the scope
  for cuts.

LANGUAGE TO WATCH:
  • "The bigger the hype, the bigger the concern" — the most quotable
    line of the panel; likely widely cited. Watch for spread to Troika.
  • K-shaped economy + equity wealth = demand pull-forward — specific
    mechanism bypassing hand-to-mouth constraints. Novel in committee
    context.
  • "If the central bank reacts too slowly, it probably gets worse" —
    implicit commentary on current rate path without being explicit.

ANALYST FLAG: YES
  → Goolsbee's r-star argument is the most analytically significant
    element of the Hoover panel. If the Troika or key voters adopt this
    framework, it signals a committee-wide revision of the neutral rate
    estimate upward — reducing the scope for rate cuts materially.
