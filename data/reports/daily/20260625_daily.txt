GROWTH
  Not a primary topic; discussed through the AI/productivity lens. He cites
  productivity running "75 basis points, one full percentage point higher"
  than pre-COVID — echoing the June 17 statement's new "productivity growth
  and capital investment are strong." Notably DROPS the May 12 consumer
  precautionary-saving downside risk.
  Direction: hawkish lean (growth as a strength/overheating vector, not a fragility).

LABOR MARKET
  Unchanged from May 12. Verbatim repeat: "the job market in my mind is stable
  without being good." Same "four horsemen of the truth" frame (unemployment,
  vacancy, layoff, hiring rates) and the same low-hire/low-fire paradox
  attributed to business uncertainty ("sit and wait"). No deterioration, no
  tightening flagged.
  Direction: neutral (genuinely unchanged).

INFLATION
  Services-persistence concern MAINTAINED: "a disturbingly high component of
  services inflation which isn't really from oil prices … isn't really from
  tariffs." HARDENS the line by refusing to look dovishly through a possible
  energy decline: "I cautioned them on the way up … I'm cautioning you on the
  way down. Don't overreact either." Discounts the one favorable goods-CPI
  print ("one month is no months"). Calls tariffs "more persistent than people
  anticipated." Tracks the committee's "supply shocks" framing.
  Direction: hawkish lean (holds the line as energy headlines improve).

MONETARY POLICY
  The real move. The May 12 conditional cut signal — "rates can come down still
  a fair amount" — is ABSENT. In its place, two hawkish-capable additions:
  (1) NEW productivity-OVERHEATING framing — expected AI productivity gains pull
  spending forward (wealth effects, trillion-dollar IPOs, data-center capex), so
  you "can easily overheat the economy in the short run before the magic of the
  productivity has arrived," drawn explicitly to the late-1990s Greenspan parallel
  that ended in "raising interest rates very rapidly." (2) His stated top worry is
  inflation PERSISTENCE and expectations de-anchoring, anchored to the 2021–22
  "transitory" error ("people already got burned once"). He also ratifies Warsh's
  removal of forward guidance ("a caffeine cleanse"; "I have always been uneasy
  with … forward guidance on a routine basis") — governance alignment, not a rate
  signal. Scenario framing: descriptive (risks named), not explicit/pre-committed.
  [Inference] The cut-signal absence is partly topic-driven (he was never asked his
  preferred rate path), so not by itself a retraction — but everything he
  volunteered leans hawkish with no offsetting cut language.
  Direction: hawkish lean.
