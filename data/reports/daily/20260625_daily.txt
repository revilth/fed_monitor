1.  WILLIAMS  |  NY Fed President  |  Troika
    "The Strategy and the Goals" — Crane's Money Fund Symposium
    Prepared remarks (not publicly delivered) — June 25, 2026
    CLASSIFICATION: Type A (Economic Outlook) + embedded Type-B operational
    section (ample reserves / RMP balance-sheet mechanics).
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  Resilient, AI-investment-led — unchanged from May 4. "The U.S. economy has so
  far absorbed these shocks and has remained resilient." Real GDP ~2-1/4% this
  year and the next two, "modestly above its potential rate of 2 percent";
  unemployment edging down to 4% by 2028. Echoes the June 17 statement's strong-
  investment language.
  Direction: neutral (a hair firmer).

LABOR MARKET
  KEY SHIFT. The soft-data slack concern Williams pressed on May 4 has receded.
  On May 4 the job-availability surveys and the NY Fed "job security gap" were
  "trending downward" and signalled "increasing labor market slack." On June 25
  they "had been flashing warning signs earlier — have also largely stabilized,
  albeit at low levels," and other measures are "stable or have modestly
  strengthened." The dovish labor leg is effectively withdrawn. He keeps the
  anchor that labor "is not adding to inflationary pressures."
  Direction: hawkish lean (the May "increasing slack" call is gone).

INFLATION
  Dovish in DIRECTION, more persistent in DURATION. Explicit call: "In coming
  quarters, however, I expect inflation readings to edge down" — tariff effects
  "mostly played out," new tariffs downplayed as net-neutral (a softening from
  May 4, where a new tariff round was an upside RISK), energy stabilizing then
  falling if Hormuz resolves, shelter slowing. BUT the return-to-2% date SLIPS:
  "land on target in 2028" vs. his May 4 "2% in 2027" — a full year later. Adds a
  third driver new vs. May: "robust demand for certain categories of technology
  goods related to the AI investment boom" (also flagged as an upside risk).
  Expectations "well anchored."
  Direction: mixed — dovish direction, mild-hawkish duration; net constructive.

MONETARY POLICY
  Troika holdline intact. Endorses the June 17 hold; "it is imperative that we
  restore [inflation] to our 2 percent longer-run goal on a sustained basis. The
  current stance of monetary policy is well positioned to do that." NO cut signal,
  NO forward guidance (consistent with Warsh's June 17 removal); closes on pure
  data-dependence. The "imperative … restore" register is statement-aligned
  (top-down echo of "will deliver price stability"), marginally firmer than May 4's
  symmetric "balance the risks" — not a new personal signal. Cut signal: ABSENT
  (unchanged). Scenario framing: descriptive (AI/Mideast risks named, no policy
  pre-commitment).
  Direction: neutral (unchanged hold).

  Operational note (Type B, no stance signal): ample-reserves framework; RMPs
  maintaining "ample" — tapered $40B/mo (Dec) → $25B → $10B (mid-May–mid-Jul);
  "stand ready to adjust … up or down." Relevant to money markets / balance-sheet
  trajectory, not the rate path.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL with a mild HAWKISH lean vs. May 4 — patient-hold Troika
anchor, but the dovish labor-slack leg is withdrawn and the 2%-target horizon
slips a year, partly offset by a confident "inflation will edge down" call.

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (May 4):        HAWKISH LEAN — drops "increasing labor market
  slack"; pushes the return-to-2% date 2027 → 2028; downplays new tariffs (dovish
  offset).
vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (May 4):     HAWKISH LEAN — same (May 4 is both his prior
  Type A and his pre-June-17 baseline).
vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:        Williams is the CONSTRUCTIVE Troika voice within
  the hawkish June 17 framing — a concrete disinflation glide path rather than the
  statement's persistence/commitment register, but no cut signal. Notably LESS
  hawkish than non-voter Goolsbee the same day (see cross-speaker note).

  • "I expect inflation readings to edge down" — clearest forward disinflation call
     from the Troika since June 17. Watch Jefferson/Warsh for echo (bottom-up;
     DISPERSED/Troika-only). Could presage a future cut bias while the statement
     still reads hawkish — a dots-vs-statement gap.
  • "largely stabilized, albeit at low levels" — withdrawal of the May labor-slack
     concern; reversible if July payrolls (Jul 3) soften.
  • "land on target in 2028" — return-to-2% horizon slipped a year vs. May.

LABOR MARKET
  Unchanged from May 12. Verbatim: "the job market in my mind is stable without

  Direction: neutral.

INFLATION

  services inflation … isn't really from oil prices … isn't really from tariffs").

  months").
  Direction: hawkish lean.

MONETARY POLICY
  The May 12 conditional cut signal ("rates can come down still a fair amount") is

  expected AI gains pull spending forward (wealth effects, trillion-dollar IPOs,
  data-center capex), so you "can easily overheat the economy in the short run
  before the magic of the productivity has arrived," drawn to the late-1990s
  Greenspan parallel that ended in rapid hikes. (2) Top worry is inflation
  PERSISTENCE / expectations de-anchoring, anchored to the 2021–22 "transitory"
  error. Ratifies Warsh's forward-guidance removal ("a caffeine cleanse").
  [Inference] cut-signal absence is partly topic-driven (never asked his rate path)
  — not a formal retraction, but everything volunteered leans hawkish.
  Direction: hawkish lean.

A notable INVERSION: the Troika member is the dove relative to the non-voter.
  • AGREE: labor is stable/resilient (no rush); AI investment is a genuine engine;
    the June 17 hold is appropriate; no cut signaled; forward guidance gone.
  • DIVERGE on AI + inflation trajectory — the key split:
      – Williams: AI demand is one of three inflation drivers, but overall
        inflation will "edge down" on a glide path to 2% (in 2028). Constructive.
      – Goolsbee: the AI boom is itself an OVERHEATING/inflation risk that could
        force rapid hikes (Greenspan-1990s parallel); top worry is persistence.
  • DIVERGE on persistence vs. disinflation: Williams expects readings to edge
    down; Goolsbee warns against "declaring loss" too early and emphasizes the
    2021–22 burn.
Read: heading toward the July 28–29 meeting, the committee's hawkish energy is
coming from the regionals, while the Troika (Williams) is quietly more sanguine on
the disinflation path — a tension the analyst should track for a dots-vs-statement
gap. Watch Jefferson/Warsh to see which side the Troika consolidates around.
