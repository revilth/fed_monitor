1.  LORIE LOGAN  |  Dallas Fed President  |  Just Voter
    "Opening remarks for moderated conversation at UTEP"
    University of Texas at El Paso, June 3, 2026
    CLASSIFICATION: Type A — Full Economic Outlook speech
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  "Economic activity remains strong." — slightly more emphatic than
  the Apr 29 statement's "expanding at a solid pace." Consumer spending
  "robust," corporate earnings "going gangbusters" (S&P 500 earnings
  +25% Q1 YoY; median company +14%). "Financial conditions are
  accommodative." — second voter in 24 hours (after Hammack Jun 2)
  to characterize conditions as permissive rather than restraining.
  AI investment booming but disinflationary benefits "uncertain"
  in timing. No compositional demand-side risk flagged.
  Direction: neutral-hawkish (accommodative conditions framing)

LABOR MARKET
  "The labor market appears stable and broadly balanced." —
  Unchanged characterization from May 1 and May 10. Unemployment
  rate 4.3%, hovered for a year. Critically: Logan reframes 50K/month
  job gains (which the Apr 29 statement calls "low") as "in line with
  the slow growth rate of the labor force" — a deliberate analytical
  divergence from the committee's softness framing. Closes with:
  "these conditions indicate that monetary policy is not restraining
  the economy." [Inference: labor + growth + accommodative conditions
  together argue current rates are insufficient, not just neutral.]
  Direction: neutral-hawkish (stable framing removes dovish leg;
  "not restraining" inference mirrors Hammack's "not restrictive")

INFLATION
  "Inflation is taking too long to return to the FOMC's 2 percent
  target." Headline PCE ~4% past 12 months; Core PCE 3.3%; Dallas
  trimmed mean 2.3% but EXPLICITLY DISCOUNTED: "my staff's research
  cautions against putting too much stock in low readings of the
  trimmed mean" — a composition effect is pulling it artificially
  low. Cleveland median PCE 2.8%; NY Fed multivariate core trend
  above 3%. Dallas Fed tariff research: tariffs affect the LEVEL of
  prices, not the ongoing inflation RATE. Bottom line — KEY:
  "inflation appears to be trending toward the mid 2's — not all
  the way back to 2 percent." First explicit above-2% inflation
  FORECAST from any voter. Prior formulation (May 1): "taking too
  long." Now: will not reach 2% under current conditions.
  Direction: HAWKISH (above-2% forecast; trimmed-mean disabled;
  tariff/energy treated as additive to pre-existing problem)

MONETARY POLICY
  "I am increasingly concerned that higher interest rates could be
  necessary later this year to fully restore price stability and
  appropriately balance both sides of the Fed's dual mandate."
  First EXPLICIT HIKE SIGNAL from Logan. Component analysis:
  "could be necessary" = conditional (not imminent); "later this
  year" = within 2026 but not "soon" (cf. Hammack: "may soon");
  "fully restore price stability" = current stance insufficient;
  "balance both sides" = hike framed as serving BOTH mandate legs.
  The symmetric framing from May 1 ("either an increase or a cut")
  has resolved hawkish: hike is now the primary concern, not one of
  two symmetric options. Scenario framing: DESCRIPTIVE (not named
  scenario labels per Waller/Hammack framework).
  Cut signal status: ABSENT. Hike signal: PRESENT — conditional,
  "later this year."
  Direction: HAWKISH (first explicit Logan hike signal; symmetric
  framing resolved to asymmetric-hawkish)

NET SIGNAL: HAWKISH — escalation from May 1 dissent. Three structural
  shifts: (1) Symmetric "either/or" → explicit conditional hike;
  (2) Uncertainty about reaching 2% → explicit forecast of settling
  in "mid 2's"; (3) "Accommodative" financial conditions + "not
  restraining" inference — policy diagnosis matches Hammack's.

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (May 1, dissent statement):   HAWKISH — May 1:
  symmetric "either an increase or a cut." Jun 3: hike is the
  explicit primary concern. Inflation framing upgraded from
  uncertainty to explicit above-2% forecast. Significant escalation.

vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (Feb 10):   HAWKISH — Feb 10: "not yet fully
  confident inflation is heading all the way back to 2 percent"
  (uncertainty framing). Jun 3: "trending toward the mid 2's —
  not all the way back to 2 percent" (explicit pessimistic forecast).
  Full trajectory: Feb 10 hawkish concern → May 1 symmetric →
  Jun 3 explicit hike-leaning.

vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:   Logan joins Waller (May 22), Cook (May 27),
  and Hammack (Jun 2) as the fourth voter to deploy explicit
  hike-signaling language post-April-29. She is the third Just Voter
  (regional president) to do so, alongside two Board Governors (Waller,
  Cook). Kashkari's Apr 29 dissent record implies a fifth hawkish hold
  at minimum. Bowman remains the single explicit dovish outlier.

LANGUAGE TO WATCH:
  • "inflation appears to be trending toward the mid 2's — not all
    the way back to 2 percent" — FIRST above-2% inflation forecast
    from any voter. If Warsh or Jefferson echo this phrase before the
    blackout, it signals the June 18 statement will not include language
    implying confidence in returning to 2%. The June 18 policy decision
    may need to address a committee that does not believe its current
    stance achieves the inflation mandate.
  • "higher interest rates could be necessary later this year" —
    conditional hike signal with "later this year" timeline vs.
    Hammack's "may soon." Two voters now have explicit hike language
    with different temporal urgency.
  • "monetary policy is not restraining the economy" — [Inference
    from the speech's joint reading of labor + growth + financial
    conditions.] Mirrors Hammack's Jun 2 "policy may not be
    sufficiently restrictive." Two voters, same diagnosis, within
    24 hours.
  • Trimmed-mean caveat — Logan disables the Dallas Fed's own 2.3%
    reading. This is the most dovish of the standard inflation metrics;
    its removal narrows the range of measures pointing toward 2%.

ANALYST FLAG: YES
  → HIKE COALITION NOW FOUR VOTERS: Waller (May 22), Cook (May 27),
    Hammack (Jun 2), Logan (Jun 3). Four of 12 voters deployed explicit
    hike-ready language in 12 days. Kashkari (Apr 29 dissent record,
    no post-FOMC transcript) likely makes five. If Warsh or Jefferson
    hold similar views, the committee has a hike majority.
  → Logan's "mid 2's, not 2 percent" forecast is the most important
    analytical claim in this speech. It implies the current stance
    is structurally insufficient — not just uncertain. Analyst should
    assess whether Logan's multi-measure analysis (discounting trimmed
    mean, citing NY Fed multivariate trend above 3%) represents the
    committee consensus view of the inflation path going into June 18.

2.  BETH M. HAMMACK  |  Cleveland Fed President  |  Just Voter
    "It Takes Two to Make an Economy Go Right"
    City Club of Cleveland, June 2, 2026
    CLASSIFICATION: Type A — Full Economic Outlook speech
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  Consumer spending rising at a moderate pace; AI business investment
  strong; Cleveland Fed SORCE indices show strengthening conditions.
  HAWKISH ADDITION: financial conditions are "supportive of growth
  rather than holding it back," and "policy may not be restrictive"
  — the real FFR has declined as inflation rose over the last few
  months. This is an argument that the current nominal rate is
  functionally insufficient in real terms.
  Direction: neutral-hawkish (financial conditions framing)

LABOR MARKET
  "The unemployment rate was 4.3 percent in April, similar to where
  it was last summer. This is right around my definition of full
  employment in the long term, when labor demand and labor supply are
  in balance." Claims and layoff notices remain low; no forward-looking
  deterioration signal. Hammack omits the Apr 29 statement's "job gains
  have remained low" framing — she characterizes 4.3% simply as full
  employment, removing the dovish labor leg.
  Direction: hawkish (full employment framing removes dovish leg)

INFLATION
  "Inflation is too high and is moving higher." Headline PCE: 3.8%
  in April; core PCE: 3.3%, "well above levels from six months or a
  year ago" — adverse trajectory, not merely elevated level. Broad-based
  price pressures; services ex-housing "stuck at an elevated rate...
  for the last two years" — structural argument against look-through.
  Inflation expectations: "sixth year of elevated inflation" may embed
  expectations; "decisive action" warranted if expectations de-anchor.
  Direction: hawkish (acceleration framing; services persistence;
  expectations urgency; "decisive action" first use in 2026)

MONETARY POLICY
  Base case: hold. Forward signal: "if recent data trends continue,
  it may soon be appropriate for policy to act to address the growing
  risks of persistently elevated inflation." Explicit diagnosis: "monetary
  policy may not be sufficiently restrictive." Urgency: "if we wait for
  definitive evidence that high inflation has become embedded, it may
  require larger policy adjustments, at greater cost" (Volcker logic).
  Scenario framing: EXPLICIT AND NAMED. Scenario 1 (hold): oil shock
  fades, balance dual mandate. Scenario 2 (hike): inflation stays
  elevated → "more restrictive monetary policy could well be needed."
  Cut signal status: ABSENT. Hike signal: PRESENT — "may soon be
  appropriate for policy to act." First conditional hike signal from
  Hammack.
  Direction: hawkish (first explicit Hammack hike signal; named scenario
  framing; real-rate diagnostic)

NET SIGNAL: HAWKISH — significant escalation from May 1 dissent.
  Five structural shifts: (1) Real rate argued to have FALLEN as
  inflation rose; (2) Inflation "moving higher," not just elevated;
  (3) Services ex-housing persistence argument against look-through;
  (4) Conditional hike signal; (5) Volcker urgency logic.

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (May 1, dissent):   HAWKISH — May 1 supported hold
  without forward hike signal. Jun 2 adds conditional hike language,
  real-rate falling argument, and "decisive action" on expectations.
vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (Mar 6):   HAWKISH — Mar 6: "around neutral,"
  symmetric risk. Jun 2: policy "may not be restrictive," hike signal
  present. Symmetric framing resolved decisively hawkish.
vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:   Third voter in 11 days with hike-ready
  language (Waller May 22, Cook May 27, Hammack Jun 2).

LANGUAGE TO WATCH:
  • "may soon be appropriate for policy to act" — temporal hike signal;
    "may soon" is closer-in than Logan's "later this year."
  • "monetary policy may not be sufficiently restrictive" — cleanest
    articulation of the hike-logic diagnostic.
  • "inflation is too high and is moving higher" — acceleration framing.
  • "decisive action" (on expectations) — new escalation phrase.

ANALYST FLAG: YES — see Logan section above for combined hike coalition
  analysis.

CROSS-SPEAKER NOTE (June 2–3)

Hammack (Jun 2) and Logan (Jun 3) deliver the same analytical diagnosis
through different analytical architectures:

FINANCIAL CONDITIONS CONVERGENCE:
  Hammack: "measures of financial conditions are supportive of growth
    rather than holding it back... policy may not be restrictive."
  Logan: "financial conditions are accommodative... these conditions
    indicate that monetary policy is not restraining the economy."
  → Independent convergence on "policy is not restrictive" within 24
    hours, by two regional presidents with different analytical styles
    (Hammack: explicit real-rate calculation; Logan: multi-measure
    inflation analysis with trimmed-mean caveat). This is not
    coordination artifact — it reflects a genuine committee-level
    assessment forming.

INFLATION FORECAST DIVERGENCE (SIGNIFICANT):
  Hammack: "inflation is too high and is moving higher" — trajectory
    is adverse; no explicit settling-point forecast.
  Logan: "inflation appears to be trending toward the mid 2's — not
    all the way back to 2 percent" — explicit above-2% forecast.
  → Logan's framing is more pessimistic about the terminal inflation
    rate under current policy. Hammack argues for urgency because of
    acceleration; Logan argues for urgency because current policy
    is structurally insufficient to achieve 2%.

HIKE TIMELINE DIVERGENCE:
  Hammack: "may soon be appropriate for policy to act" — short horizon.
  Logan: "could be necessary later this year" — medium horizon (but
    within 2026, before the blackout-end for multiple remaining
    meetings). Two voters, both hawkish, with different speed assessments.
