LABOR MARKET
  Williams introduces a new framing layer on top of the committee's
  "little changed" stabilization language: "Lately, the labor market has
  been displaying conflicting signs." He splits hard data (stabilizing:
  unemployment rate, payroll growth, tightness index) from soft data, where
  "some of the soft data suggest a labor market that continues to gradually
  soften." This hard/soft split is Williams' analytical contribution —
  it is NOT in the FOMC statement and was NOT in his Mar 30 speech. This
  is the first labor downside signal from any Troika member in 2026 that
  goes beyond the committee's official language.
  Direction: mild dovish lean vs. Mar 30 — new soft-data concern added.

INFLATION
  Williams maintains all prior anchors: no second-round tariff effects,
  underlying inflation (ex-imports) moving in the right direction,
  expectations well anchored. He concretizes the energy shock's breadth
  ("higher airfares, groceries, fertilizer") without a single killer
  hawkish phrase. NEW: he explicitly names the stagflation tail risk for
  the first time — "The conflict could also result in a large supply shock
  that simultaneously raises inflation and dampens economic activity."
  His 2026 inflation forecast is revised up again, with the upper end
  now at 3.0% (from ~2.75% on Mar 30, from 2.5% on Mar 3).
  Direction: mild hawkish vs. Mar 30 — upper forecast bound raised;
  stagflation tail risk named.

MONETARY POLICY
  "Well positioned to balance the risks" — near-verbatim FOMC statement
  formula. The Mar 3 cut signal ("further reductions will eventually be
  warranted") remains absent for the third consecutive speech, confirming
  the Mar 30 retraction was deliberate and durable. No new directional
  guidance in either direction.
  Direction: unchanged.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL — slight dovish lean on labor offset by slight
  hawkish lean on inflation; cut signal durably retracted

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (Mar 30):    LABOR: mild dovish (soft-data softening
  added). INFLATION: mild hawkish (forecast upper bound raised to 3.0%;
  stagflation risk named). MONETARY POLICY: unchanged (cut signal still
  absent). Net: no policy change, but analytical framework expanded
  in both directions.

vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (Mar 3):  HAWKISH overall — inflation forecast up
  from 2.5% to 2.75-3.0%; explicit cut signal gone; stagflation tail
  risk introduced. The full post-FOMC arc is a deliberate hawkish
  recalibration driven by the Middle East energy shock.

vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:     Williams remains in the Troika's neutral
  center alongside Jefferson. His "conflicting signs" labor framing is
  slightly more cautious than Jefferson's "roughly in balance + susceptible
  to shocks." Together, Williams and Jefferson define the committee's
  current analytical center of gravity: patient hold with dual-sided risk
  acknowledgment and no cut timeline.
