KEY ANALYTICAL QUESTIONS FOR JUNE 18

1. HIKE OR HOLD?
   Explicit hike-ready language: Waller, Cook, Hammack, Logan
   (4 of 12 voters). Kashkari's Apr 29 dissent = inferred 5th.
   A majority requires Warsh + at minimum Jefferson or Williams.
   Warsh has not spoken publicly since confirmation (37 days).
   His first speech post-blackout will define the outcome.

2. LOGAN'S "MID 2'S" FORECAST
   Logan's explicit forecast that inflation will settle in the
   "mid 2's — not all the way back to 2 percent" is the most
   consequential new analytical claim in the pre-blackout window.
   If the committee adopts this as its baseline: holding at
   4.25-4.50% while inflation is forecast to stay structurally
   above 2% is inconsistent with the mandate. Either rates must
   rise, or the easing bias must be explicitly removed, or a
   new communication framework is needed.

3. EASING BIAS LANGUAGE
   Bowman is the only voter explicitly defending the Apr 29
   easing bias language. The other 10 speakers in the post-FOMC
   window have avoided echoing it or have argued against it.
   The bias may be removed at June 18 regardless of the rate
   decision.

4. WARSH UNCERTAINTY PREMIUM
   36+ days as Chair with zero policy speeches. The market
   has no Warsh signal to price. This is an unusual and
   potentially significant uncertainty premium heading into
   a live meeting. Warsh's post-blackout statement (if any)
   will be the first time the actual policy principal speaks.
