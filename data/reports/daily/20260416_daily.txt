━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  Williams expects real GDP growth of 2 to 2.5% for 2026, with growth
  supported by fiscal policy tailwinds, favorable financial conditions,
  and AI investment. He notes modest downward revision from his March 30
  range ("close to 2.5%") to the current range ("2 to 2.5%"), reflecting
  energy shock drag. The NY metro housing market remains strong, with
  elevated demand and tight inventories; office return has reached 80-90%
  of pre-pandemic transit ridership. No recession concern in his baseline.
  Direction: neutral — solid but slightly trimmed outlook.

LABOR MARKET
  Williams adds a new framing layer on top of the committee's "little
  changed" language: "Lately, the labor market has been displaying
  conflicting signs." He explicitly splits hard data — unemployment rate,
  payroll growth, and NY Fed Labor Market Tightness Index all stabilizing
  — from soft data, where "some of the soft data suggest a labor market
  that continues to gradually soften." The Conference Board and NY Fed
  consumer surveys on job availability and job-finding expectations have
  continued to trend downward. This hard/soft split is his own analytical
  contribution, not in the FOMC statement and not in his Mar 30 speech.
  It is the first labor downside signal from any Troika member in 2026
  that goes beyond the committee's official language.
  Direction: mild dovish lean vs. Mar 30 — soft-data softening concern
  added for the first time.

INFLATION
  All prior anchors maintained: no second-round tariff effects, underlying
  inflation (ex-imports) moving in the right direction, expectations well
  anchored. He concretizes the energy shock's breadth with specifics —
  "higher airfares, groceries, fertilizer" — reinforcing that the pass-
  through is broad, not just pump prices. His 2026 inflation forecast is
  revised up for the second time: upper bound now 3.0% (from ~2.75% on
  Mar 30, from 2.5% on Mar 3). New this speech: he describes the
  stagflation tail risk, saying the conflict "could also result in a large
  supply shock that simultaneously raises inflation and dampens economic
  activity." He frames this descriptively as a risk scenario rather than
  labeling it explicitly as a scenario. (Compare Waller Apr 17, who
  structures this as an explicit adverse scenario with a named policy
  response — a more deliberate framing than Williams here.)
  Direction: mild hawkish vs. Mar 30 — upper forecast bound raised;
  stagflation risk described.

MONETARY POLICY
  Near-verbatim FOMC formula: "well positioned to balance the risks." The
  Mar 3 cut signal ("further reductions will eventually be warranted")
  remains absent for the third consecutive speech — the retraction is
  confirmed durable. No new directional guidance in either direction.
  Direction: unchanged.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL — slight dovish lean on labor offset by slight
  hawkish lean on inflation; cut signal durably absent

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (Mar 30):    GROWTH: unchanged. LABOR: mild dovish —
  soft-data softening added; more cautious than Mar 30's "no signs of
  deterioration." INFLATION: mild hawkish — upper forecast bound raised
  to 3.0%; stagflation risk described. MONETARY POLICY: unchanged.
vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (Mar 3):  HAWKISH overall — inflation forecast up
  from 2.5% to 2.75-3.0%; cut signal deliberately retracted; stagflation
  risk introduced. The full post-FOMC arc is a deliberate hawkish
  recalibration driven by the energy shock. On labor, the Mar 3
  "promising signs of stabilization" has softened to "conflicting signs"
  with soft-data deterioration — a dovish move on one dimension.
vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:     Troika center alongside Jefferson. "Conflicting
  signs" is slightly more cautious on labor than Jefferson's "roughly in
  balance + susceptible to shocks." Together they define the analytical
  center: patient hold, dual-risk, no cut timeline.

  • Soft data softening (Conference Board, NY Fed surveys) — not yet in
    committee language. Watch the April 29 statement for any labor upgrade.
  • Stagflation risk described (descriptively here; explicitly as a named

  • Cut signal ABSENT (3rd consecutive) — confirmed durable. Track whether
