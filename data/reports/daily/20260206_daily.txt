━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROWTH
  GDP grew ~2.2% in 2025; Jefferson modestly upgrades 2026 forecast
  to ~2.2% based on continued resilience. AI investment and deregulation
  could support productivity and growth; fourth-quarter effects of the
  government shutdown were a drag but fading. No growth concern.
  Direction: neutral — solid, untroubling backdrop.

LABOR MARKET
  "I see the overall labor market as roughly in balance, with a
  low-hiring, low-firing environment prevailing." Unemployment 4.4%;
  payrolls declined in Q4 but private sector held at +29K/month.
  "Downside risks to employment remain, but my baseline is for the
  unemployment rate to hold approximately steady throughout this year."
  Direction: neutral — stable baseline with acknowledged downside risks.

INFLATION
  "The disinflationary process to resume this year once increased
  tariffs pass through more fully to prices." Strong "look through"
  doctrine: tariffs are one-time; anchored expectations limit
  second-round effects. "A reasonable base case that the effects of
  tariffs on inflation will not be long-lasting." No energy shock
  yet — his baseline is straightforward disinflation resumption.
  Direction: neutral-dovish — confident disinflation will resume.

MONETARY POLICY
  "The current policy stance is well positioned to address the risks
  to both sides of our dual mandate." He notes the policy rate is
  "broadly in the range of neutral" — implying cuts are not urgently
  needed since policy is not restrictive. No cut signal; "well
  positioned" formula. Notably: he does NOT echo Williams' Mar 3
  cut signal (that comes later); Jefferson is more measured.
  Direction: neutral — stable hold; rates near neutral.

NET SIGNAL: NEUTRAL — stable hold; no cut signal; tariffs one-time;
  labor stable

vs. PRIOR SPEECH (Jan 16): MARGINAL — Jan 16 had more explicit
  "extent and timing of additional adjustments" language; Feb 6
  replaces this with "well positioned" stable formula.
vs. PRE-FOMC SPEECH (Jan 16): Near-identical on labor and inflation.

vs. COMMITTEE SINCE FOMC:  Troika center. Slightly more measured
