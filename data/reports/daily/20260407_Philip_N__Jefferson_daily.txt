LABOR MARKET
  Jefferson's verdict is unchanged: "I see the labor market as roughly
  in balance" — but he again appends "susceptible to adverse shocks,"
  a qualifier he first added on Mar 26 and now repeats, making it a
  durable characterization. Despite a strong March jobs headline (+178K),
  he deliberately emphasizes the soft 3-month average (~70K) and adds
  explicit caution: "I remain cautious about that assessment [of
  stabilization]." One good data point will not move him.
  Direction: mild dovish lean — downside risk now consistently flagged.

INFLATION
  He states his prior expectation ("disinflationary process would resume
  once higher tariffs are no longer pushing up consumer prices"), then
  immediately concedes: "The recent increase in energy prices, however,
  will apply some upward pressure on headline inflation, at least in the
  near term. The ongoing trade policy uncertainty and geopolitical tensions
  pose upside risk to my inflation forecast." The phrase "upside risk to
  my inflation forecast" appeared first on Mar 26 and is now durable —
  a personal admission that his baseline has deteriorated.
  Direction: slight hawkish lean vs. pre-FOMC; stable vs. Mar 26.
