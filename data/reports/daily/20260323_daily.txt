GROWTH
  Not addressed explicitly. Context is entirely the energy/inflation
  outlook. Direction: neutral.

LABOR MARKET
  Unemployment stable; rate-based measures (unemployment, layoff,
  vacancy) at levels "closer to full employment than we are on the
  inflation side." Key statement: "At the moment I think the
  inflation's got to be a little ahead of the employment."
  → Explicit asymmetric ranking: inflation takes priority over labor
    in his current framework. Goes beyond committee's "dual risk."
  Direction: neutral on labor data; hawkish on priority ranking.

INFLATION
  "We stopped making progress on the inflation front and at best it
  stalled out and started inching its way back up. So we've been
  waiting for that to have proved transitory to have gone away and
  to pile a new inflation shock on top of this before that one has
  left the scene. This is going to be a tough moment."
  → Cumulative-shocks framing: prior inflation still elevated +
    new energy shock = compounding problem. Consistent with his
    Mar 7 stagflation naming.
  "The price of gasoline specifically has a very high impact on
  individuals' expectations of what inflation is going to be over
  the next year." → Gasoline → expectations transmission as his
  specific mechanism for contagion concern.
  Direction: hawkish — cumulative shocks; expectations vulnerability.

MONETARY POLICY
  Discloses December 2025 dissent (voted against the Dec cut):
  "I dissented at the last meeting of the year because we did not
  have the data to show that inflation was going away." His hawkish
  lean in 2026 predates the energy shock — it is not solely a
  reaction to recent events.
  "I remained fairly optimistic that by the end of 26 rates could
  go down, but I wanted to see proof that we're back on an inflation
  headed to 2%. This definitely throws a wrench into the plans."
  → End-2026 cut timeline now under threat.
  Direction: hawkish — explicit inflation-first; prior dissenter;
  end-2026 cuts conditional and threatened.

NET SIGNAL: HAWKISH — inflation > labor explicitly; Dec 2025
  dissent confirmed; end-2026 cut timeline under pressure
